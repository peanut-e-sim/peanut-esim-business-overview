1.Who We Are

Peanut eSIM LLC ("Peanut eSIM", "we", "us", "our") is a limited liability company organized under the laws of the State of Delaware, United States of America, with mailing address [to be inserted]. We operate a global travel-eSIM resale service through our mobile applications, website, and supported in-app billing channels.

For the purposes of (a) the EU/EEA General Data Protection Regulation 2016/679 ("GDPR"), (b) the UK GDPR and Data Protection Act 2018, (c) the UAE Federal Decree-Law No. 45 of 2021 on the Protection of Personal Data ("UAE PDPL") and its Executive Regulation, and (d) other applicable data-protection laws (collectively, "Data Protection Law"), Peanut eSIM LLC acts as the data controller of personal data we process about you, except where we expressly act as a processor on behalf of a business customer (for example, an airline placing a bulk crew order).

2.What Data We Collect

We collect the following categories of personal data:

2.1 Account & Profile Data

  • Required: email address, password hash (we never store passwords in plaintext), country of residence.
  • Optional: first and last name, mobile phone number, preferred language, profile photo, occupation tag (e.g., "airline crew").

2.2 Payment Data

We do not store full primary account numbers (PANs), card CVVs, or full bank account numbers. Payment is processed by:

  • Stripe, Inc. for direct web/app card payments (Stripe stores the card; we receive a tokenized reference and the last four digits, card brand, expiry, country of issuance, and a risk score);
  • Apple Inc. via Apple In-App Purchase (we receive only an opaque transaction identifier and receipt);
  • Google LLC via Google Play Billing (we receive only a purchase token and order ID).

2.3 Device & Technical Data

  • Device identifier (Apple IDFV / Android Advertising ID where you have granted permission; otherwise a randomly generated app-instance ID);
  • Device model, operating system and version, app version, screen language and locale;
  • eSIM compatibility information (EID — eSIM Identifier — only when you initiate installation, and only to provision the profile);
  • IP address (truncated for analytics, retained in full only for fraud-prevention logs).

2.4 Usage Data

  • Plans purchased: SKU, destination country/region, data quota, validity period, price, currency, purchase timestamp;
  • Country of consumption (the country code reported by the partner MNO during your session);
  • Aggregate data consumption (we receive total MB used; we do not see the URLs you visit, DNS queries, the content of your communications, or the names of the apps you use);
  • Support interactions (subject line, body, attachments, timestamps).

2.5 Marketing & Communications Data

  • Newsletter opt-in status (separate, explicit opt-in — never bundled with the account creation tick-box);
  • Email open/click events from marketing emails (only when you have opted in);
  • Referral codes you have used or shared.

2.6 Inferred & Aggregated Data

We may generate aggregated, de-identified, or statistical data from the above categories (e.g., "average UAE-resident customer purchases 2.4 plans per year"). De-identified data is not personal data and is not subject to this policy.

5.International Data Transfers

Because we are a Delaware-incorporated company serving a global customer base, your personal data will be transferred to, stored in, and processed in countries other than the country in which you provided it. Recipient countries currently include:

  • United States (corporate headquarters; Stripe; Vercel; AWS US regions; PostHog; Intercom);
  • Japan (Samurai Wifi headquarters and operations; co-founder support);
  • United Arab Emirates (primary customer-support hub for the Gulf region);
  • Ireland & Germany (AWS EU regions; sub-processor EU support);
  • Singapore (AWS APAC region for low-latency MENA / Asia routing);
  • India (selected contracted support agents).

For transfers of personal data out of the EEA / UK, we rely on (a) Standard Contractual Clauses issued by the European Commission (Module 1 — controller-to-controller — and Module 2 — controller-to-processor — as applicable), as updated by Commission Implementing Decision (EU) 2021/914, together with the UK Addendum issued by the ICO; and (b) where required, supplementary technical measures including in-transit and at-rest encryption.

For transfers out of the UAE, we rely on the cross-border transfer mechanisms permitted under UAE PDPL Articles 22 and 23, including transfer to jurisdictions with adequate protection as designated by the UAE Data Office, or under written contractual safeguards equivalent to PDPL standards.

7.Your Rights — GDPR (EU / EEA / UK)

If you are a resident of the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or Switzerland, you have the following rights under GDPR (and the equivalent provisions of UK GDPR and Swiss FADP):

  • Right of access (Art. 15) — to confirm whether we process your data and obtain a copy;
  • Right to rectification (Art. 16) — to correct inaccurate or incomplete data;
  • Right to erasure / "right to be forgotten" (Art. 17) — subject to legal-retention obligations;
  • Right to restriction of processing (Art. 18);
  • Right to data portability (Art. 20) — to receive your data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format and have it transmitted to another controller;
  • Right to object (Art. 21) — including an absolute right to object to direct marketing;
  • Rights related to automated decision-making (Art. 22) — although we do not currently engage in qualifying automated decision-making;
  • Right to withdraw consent (Art. 7(3)) at any time, without affecting the lawfulness of pre-withdrawal processing;
  • Right to lodge a complaint with the data-protection supervisory authority of your habitual residence, place of work, or place of the alleged infringement (Art. 77).

To exercise these rights, email privacy@[domain]. We will respond within one month of receipt, with a possible two-month extension for complex requests (Art. 12(3)). We may need to verify your identity before fulfilling a request.

8.Your Rights — UAE PDPL

If you are located in the United Arab Emirates (excluding the DIFC and ADGM free zones, which have their own data-protection regimes), the UAE Federal Decree-Law No. 45 of 2021 on the Protection of Personal Data ("UAE PDPL") and its Executive Regulation grant you broadly equivalent rights, including:

  • Right to access the personal data we hold about you (Art. 13);
  • Right to request correction of inaccurate data (Art. 14);
  • Right to request deletion of data, subject to applicable retention requirements (Art. 15);
  • Right to restrict or object to processing (Art. 16);
  • Right to request transfer of your data in a structured format (Art. 17);
  • Right to withdraw consent to processing that relies on consent (Art. 6);
  • Right to file a complaint with the UAE Data Office if you believe your rights have been violated (Art. 22 of the Executive Regulation).

Customers in the DIFC may exercise equivalent rights under the DIFC Data Protection Law 2020. Customers in the ADGM may rely on the ADGM Data Protection Regulations 2021. To exercise any of the above, contact privacy@[domain]; we will respond within thirty (30) days.

Confirm whether to appoint a UAE-resident representative (recommended for non-resident controllers under PDPL Executive Regulation), and whether DIFC/ADGM-specific addenda are needed for residents of those free zones (their data-protection statutes are separate from the federal PDPL).

11.How We Secure Your Data

We apply a defense-in-depth security program that includes, at a high level: encryption of personal data in transit using TLS 1.2+; encryption at rest for primary databases and backups using industry-standard symmetric ciphers; role-based access controls with the principle of least privilege; mandatory multi-factor authentication for all staff accessing production systems; centralized audit logging; independent vulnerability testing at least annually; and an employee privacy & security training program. We deliberately do not publish granular technical detail in this Policy in order to limit the risk of providing a roadmap to attackers.

However, no system is completely secure. You play an important role in protecting your account: choose a strong unique password, enable two-factor authentication where offered, and notify us immediately at security@[domain] if you suspect unauthorized access.

12.Data Breach Notification

In the event of a personal data breach that is likely to result in a risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons, we will:

  1. Notify the competent supervisory authority (the lead EU supervisory authority for affected EEA residents, the ICO for UK residents, the UAE Data Office for affected UAE residents, and other relevant regulators) without undue delay, and where feasible within seventy-two (72) hours of becoming aware of the breach, per GDPR Art. 33 and UAE PDPL Executive Regulation;
  2. Communicate the breach directly to affected data subjects without undue delay, where the breach is likely to result in a high risk to their rights and freedoms, per GDPR Art. 34;
  3. Document the facts, effects, and remedial action taken in a written incident record retained for at least three years.

Notifications will include, at minimum: the nature of the breach, the categories and approximate number of data subjects affected, the likely consequences, and the measures taken or proposed to address it and to mitigate its possible adverse effects.

The 72-hour clock and the high-risk threshold for individual notice are subject to specific procedural rules in each jurisdiction. The UAE PDPL Executive Regulation may impose shorter or different reporting requirements than GDPR; California (CCPA / CPRA) and Apple's developer agreement have their own breach-notice rules. Confirm the breach-response playbook with counsel before launch.
