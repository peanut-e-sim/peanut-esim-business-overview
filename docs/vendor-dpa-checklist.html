Vendor DPA Checklist

Working register of every third party that Processes personal data on behalf of Peanut eSIM LLC, the DPA that applies to each, and the current signing status. Use this page when onboarding new processors or completing privacy impact assessments.

Owner: Privacy & Compliance, Peanut eSIM LLC
Review cadence: quarterly
Vendors enumerated: 17
Status: Pre-launch

For each vendor below we record (a) which standard contract applies — the vendor’s own DPA, our vendor DPA template, or no separate DPA where the platform terms cover Processing — (b) the personal data the vendor receives, and (c) the next action to reach a signed position before product launch. Pillars: SIGNED when fully executed and filed; TO DO when action is still required; N/A when no separate DPA exists and the platform agreement covers Processing; ACTION CRITICAL for high-volume processors blocking launch.

This register is a working document. Counsel should validate (i) the legal basis for transfers to each non-EEA vendor (adequacy vs SCCs vs derogation), (ii) whether any vendor below is in fact a joint controller rather than a processor (notably analytics and ad platforms), and (iii) whether the volume of data we send to Mixpanel and Google Analytics 4 triggers a transfer impact assessment under Schrems II.

9. Working notes

The register above is intentionally fenced to vendors that handle personal data on our behalf. Vendors that do not Process personal data (e.g., commodity SaaS without user data, public cloud documentation services) are tracked in a separate operational tooling list and are out of scope for this DPA register.

Each new vendor must, before being granted production access to personal data:

  • Be added to this register with a category, role, and data-processed description;
  • Have either (a) their published DPA reviewed and accepted, or (b) our DPA template attached and counter-signed;
  • Be added to the internal Record of Processing Activities (RoPA) within five business days;
  • Be referenced in the Privacy Policy if the vendor is named or category of vendor is materially new.

The Privacy & Compliance owner reviews this register quarterly and confirms in writing that the list matches what is actually deployed in production. Vendor de-onboarding triggers Section 12 deletion certification under whichever DPA applies.
