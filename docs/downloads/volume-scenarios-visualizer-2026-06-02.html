5The hidden assumption — tier mix evolves at scale

The model holds a flat $12.48 across all six scenarios. But real user behavior changes as the platform matures. Early users try Economy. Loyal users climb to Premium, Business, Cockpit. The result: the model is conservative — it understates the endgame by $3.2M/yr at 100K users.

Economy mix shrinks as users mature

From 65% Economy (Beta) to 25% Economy (100K) — as users climb the ladder, blended profit per user rises from $11.47 to $15.20.

Model assumes flat $12.48 throughout. Realistic evolution produces $15.20/user at 100K. The gap is $3.2M/yr at full scale.

Anti-patterns we are NOT doing: No "upgrade!" banners everywhere (users mute them). No cost comparison hidden. No forced upgrade after X days. No penalty for downgrading. One-way ratchet — climb by choice only, never by coercion.

8Implementation roadmap

Build in order of payoff-per-effort. Nudge #1 (refuel trigger) is the highest-leverage feature in the entire product.

Nudges #1 + #3 — Refuel trigger + Pool 80%

The two highest-leverage nudges. Nudge #1 converts Economy PAYG users to Premium before they realize they could have subscribed all along. Nudge #3 catches users at peak frustration and presents the upgrade exactly when they want it.

Nudges #2 + #4 — Roster sync + 3-month anniversary

Nudge #2 converts roster-upload users to Roster Bundle immediately — very visible payoff because it requires the roster feature to already be live. Nudge #4 starts the Cockpit pipeline for proven loyalists.

9The honest reads — and one ask for Akito

Two things to keep front-of-mind when sharing this model with anyone.

OPEX = $0 today (model placeholder only)

The model carries $1.80/user/mo as an operating cost placeholder. As of 2026-06-02, there is no real OPEX. Stripe fees, AWS, support — none of it is actually being spent yet. At $0 real OPEX, gross profit = net profit. Do not present the placeholder-OPEX net numbers to investors as real — they are a stress-test input, not an actual expense line.

The 5K to 20K jump is the hardest part

Emirates has ~22,000 cabin crew. At 25% penetration we hit ~5,500 users — close to the Month 24 target. Getting to 20,000 users means leaving Emirates and onboarding Etihad, JAL, ANA, Qatar Airways. Each airline is a new partnership, new onboarding push, new word-of-mouth network from scratch.

This is the moment we stop being "the Emirates eSIM" and become "Crew Universal eSIM." That positioning shift is the hardest transition in the entire 100K plan.

Ask for Akito — airline expansion sequencing

The model treats all users identically regardless of which airline they fly for. But the cost of acquiring each airline cohort varies enormously — some need formal partnerships, some are word-of-mouth spillover. Before we build the multi-airline version of the deck:

  • Which airline after Emirates has the best crew connectivity to Akito's network?
  • Is Etihad crew reachable through the same layover-chat channels as Emirates?
  • What's the realistic timeline from "Emirates at 5K" to "Etihad cohort added"?
  • Does JAL/ANA crew community require Japanese-language onboarding? (Akito leads this.)
