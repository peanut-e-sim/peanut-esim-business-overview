👤Who it's for

The persona behind this plan

The most senior, most data-hungry crew: pilots, pursers, and long-haul First Class crew who want unlimited-style data with the confidence of 80 GB. Example: Captain Tanaka, flying DXB–NRT–DXB and DXB–SYD routes monthly, needs guaranteed data for navigation apps, family video calls from hotel, and hotspot sharing with juniors. First Suite exists as the price anchor — making Business and Premium look great by comparison. The problem is severe: at 60% usage (48 GB consumed), COGS are $64.32 against a $49.99 price tag.

🎛️Sensitivity — what actually fixes this?

Current loses $14.33/user. Options: raise price to $79.99, cut pool to 40 GB, or both.

To become profitable: raise price to $79.99 OR cut pool to 40 GB. At $79.99 with 80 GB pool: $79.99 − $64.32 = $15.67 net (20% margin — marginal but viable). At 40 GB pool: 24 GB consumed × $1.34 = $32.16 COGS → $17.83 net at $49.99. Preferred fix: cut pool to 40 GB and reposition First Suite as "40 GB + unlimited rollover" — the rollover story is more compelling than the raw pool size.

🔍The honest read

RED FLAG: Loses $14.33/user/month at 60% consumption. Pool is too generous for the price point. Must rebalance before launch. First Suite's "anchor" role in the pricing ladder is valuable — it makes Business look like great value. But an anchor that loses $14/user has no role in a sustainable business. The fix is non-negotiable: either cut the pool to 40 GB, or raise price to $79.99. Raising price reinforces premium positioning (First Suite should feel exclusive), while keeping 80 GB makes the plan defensible for power users. Whatever the decision, it must be made before launch — this cannot go live as-is.
