## Verdict: SUGGESTIVE — not direct hits, but real signal in 3 of 6 vectors

No confirmed enforcement action was found that directly names a foreign eSIM brand selling to UAE consumers. However, three vectors show material escalation that undermines the original assumption of benign non-enforcement.

## Finding 1 — TDRA: Airalo app/website blocked in UAE (CONFIRMED, July 2024)

**Signal strength: HIGH**
**Date confirmed: July 8, 2024 (Airalo Facebook announcement)**

Airalo publicly confirmed its app and website became inaccessible inside the UAE. They did not name the authority responsible, describing only "circumstances we are working relentlessly to solve." Independent user reports (April 2024, April 2025) confirmed the same for Nomad and Holafly — all three inaccessible on Etisalat's network.

This is NOT a formal published TDRA enforcement ruling, but the mechanism is network-level blocking coordinated by TDRA/licensed operators. No TDRA press release was located — they appear to enforce via operator instruction, not public announcement.

**Key nuance for Peanut:** The blocking applies to purchasing/activating from inside the UAE. Pre-installed eSIMs continue functioning. Peanut's crew-first model (users buy eSIM in their home country or while abroad) is outside the enforcement perimeter as currently structured — but this is a 1-step escalation away from consumer-targeting.

## Finding 2 — FTA: Corporate Tax Permanent Establishment risk is now ACTIVE, not theoretical

**Signal strength: HIGH**
**Effective: June 2023 (Corporate Tax Law); enforcement ramp-up visible in 2024–2025**

UAE Corporate Tax Law Article 11(3)(b) deems a foreign entity UAE-tax-resident if it is "effectively managed and controlled" from the UAE. A UAE-resident director habitually concluding contracts, making strategic decisions, or managing the company from UAE = Permanent Establishment (PE) trigger.

FTA enforcement data (2024): 93,000 inspection visits — a 135% year-on-year increase. Strategy 2023–2026 is explicitly risk-based. First corporate tax returns due July 2025; enforcement window for FY2024 is now open.

For a Delaware LLC with both founders based in UAE (Balraj + Akito): the "dependent agent PE" test and the "management and control" test are both live risks. There are no published case names (FTA does not publish case law publicly), but the framework and enforcement machinery are fully operational.

**New 2025 escalation:** Cabinet Decision No. 55 of 2025 + FTA Decision 5 of 2025 introduced updated PE rules and enforcement mechanisms effective retrospectively from 1 June 2023. October 2025 Cabinet changes to penalty regime take effect April 14, 2026 — meaning Peanut's Phase 0–1 period falls squarely in the new stricter regime.

## Finding 3 — Banking: Mercury card shipments to UAE paused; non-resident LLC environment tightened materially in 2025

**Signal strength: MEDIUM-HIGH**
**Date: Active as of 2024–2025**

Mercury announced in mid-2024 that physical card shipments to UAE are paused "due to the ongoing conflict in the region." UAE is NOT on Mercury's account-closure prohibited list (that list covers OFAC/FATF high-risk jurisdictions — Nigeria, Zimbabwe, Cuba, etc.). However:

1. Mercury and other US neobanks tightened non-US-resident LLC account requirements significantly in 2025. Many non-residents report sudden account closures and extended KYC demands.
2. UAE residents with Delaware LLCs are not prohibited outright, but are in a higher-scrutiny bucket.
3. Physical debit cards cannot ship to UAE addresses — operations must run on virtual cards, which creates friction for any team-card issuance.

**No case found** of a UAE resident losing a US LLC account specifically for UAE-sourced income or FTA reasons. Risk is banking-operational rather than regulatory-enforcement.

## Finding 4 — Consumer Protection: New framework enacted, but no enforcement actions found against foreign digital services

**Signal strength: LOW-MEDIUM**
**Effective: 2024–2025**

Cabinet Resolution No. 200 of 2025 covers e-commerce violations. Federal Decree-Law No. 6 of 2025 (Central Bank) extends consumer protection to fintech and virtual asset services with fines up to AED 1 billion. The Ministry of Economy's framework includes fines up to AED 1 million for consumer violations.

No specific enforcement case was located targeting a foreign eSIM or digital-service company selling to UAE consumers. Enforcement appears to concentrate on UAE-licensed entities. The practical question of whether a foreign company with no UAE entity is reachable under consumer protection law is unresolved — the legislation is written broadly but enforcement extraterritoriality has not been tested publicly.

## Finding 5 — AML/Central Bank: New AML law enacted 2025; cross-border transaction scrutiny elevated

**Signal strength: MEDIUM**
**Effective: Federal Law No. 10 of 2025 (new AML law)**

The UAE replaced its 2018 AML law in 2025. New requirements include real-time transaction monitoring for financial institutions and heightened scrutiny on cross-border payments. An April 2026 Central Bank update strengthened implementation.

No case found of a UAE resident having bank accounts frozen for receiving Delaware LLC income. However: the structural risk is real for founders receiving eSIM subscription payments routed through a US LLC back to personal UAE bank accounts, particularly at scale. UAE banks routinely ask for source-of-funds documentation on irregular inbound foreign transfers.

## Top 3 Most Concerning Signals

1. **TDRA app blocking (July 2024):** Airalo — the world's largest eSIM provider — was blocked inside the UAE without a public regulatory ruling. This is the enforcement template. If Peanut grows visible inside the UAE market, the same mechanism applies. The absence of a public ruling is not reassuring; it means there is no appeals process or due process signal.

2. **FTA Permanent Establishment risk is now operational:** Corporate Tax came into force June 2023. The first full enforcement year is FY2024, returns due July 2025. A UAE-based founding team managing a Delaware LLC = textbook PE risk. The new April 2026 penalty regime means fines are higher going forward. This is Phase 0 risk, not Phase 2.

3. **UAE AML + bank source-of-funds scrutiny:** As eSIM subscription revenue scales through the US LLC and lands in UAE personal/business accounts, UAE banks will seek source-of-funds documentation. Federal Law No. 10 of 2025 strengthens this. Not immediately blocking but operationally material at >AED ~50–100K monthly inbound.

## Has the enforcement environment changed since the original assumption?

**Yes — materially worse in two of six vectors:**

- The TDRA blocking of Airalo (July 2024) moved the eSIM provider risk from "theoretical" to "demonstrated." The original assumption that TDRA does not enforce against foreign eSIM brands is now factually incorrect — it enforces via network blocking, just not via published rulings.
- UAE Corporate Tax enforcement is now fully live. The original assumption likely pre-dated or assumed a slow enforcement ramp. The FTA ran 93,000 inspections in 2024 and has activated risk-based audit infrastructure. A UAE-resident-managed Delaware LLC is a red-flag pattern in their risk models.

---

## Risks Peanut May Not Have Anticipated

1. **No due process on TDRA blocking.** If Peanut's app/website gets blocked inside UAE, there is no published appeal mechanism. Airalo still doesn't explain "the circumstances." Plan for out-of-UAE purchase flow from day one (not as a fallback).

2. **UAE Corporate Tax registration obligation triggers within 9 months of PE existence.** If either founder is habitually concluding contracts or making strategic decisions from UAE for the Delaware LLC, the registration clock is already ticking. This is a compliance obligation, not a choice.

3. **Mercury physical card gap.** Team can't receive Mercury physical cards in UAE. Virtual-card-only operations for the foreseeable future. Budget for this in ops planning.

4. **New AML law creates documentation burden at scale.** Build a source-of-funds paper trail from day one (LLC → Wise/Payoneer business account → UAE bank with clear labeling). This is free to do now and expensive to reconstruct later.
