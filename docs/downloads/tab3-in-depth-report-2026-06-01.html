Tab 3 — Per-Tier Unit Economics: In-Depth Report

Prepared 2026-06-01 · Captures every column, formula, per-tier result, and downstream connection from the bite-by-bite walkthrough · Owners: Balraj + Akito

Tab 3 in one sentence

Tab 3 translates the retail prices from Tab 1 and the consumption behavior from Tab 2 into a single per-USER profit number for each tier. It is the bridge between architecture and money. Every downstream scaling tab (Volume Scenarios, 24-Month Rollout, LTV-CAC) multiplies these per-user numbers by user counts.

This document captures the full structure of Tab 3 — Per-Tier Unit Economics of the Peanut eSIM P&L Model. Tab 3 answers a single question: "For one subscriber on each plan, in one month, how much profit do we make?" The walkthrough explains all 13 columns (3 column groups), the locked per-tier results, the strategic insights about which tier wins, and the downstream tabs that depend on these numbers. Every tier is profitable — the ladder ranges from 62% (Cockpit) to 73% (Economy/Refuel) gross margin.

Why OPEX is $1.80

The $1.80 figure comes from Tab 1 Section 5 (Operating Cost per User per Month). It bundles payment processing ($1.20 mixed Stripe/Apple IAP), customer support amortized ($0.30), cloud/API ($0.15), SMS/OTP ($0.05), hosting ($0.10). This is the same number applied across every tier — the assumption being that ops cost scales linearly per active user, regardless of plan size.

4. Strategic insights

4.1 First Suite is the hero

At $33.03 gross profit / user / month, First Suite generates more absolute profit than any other tier. The reason is structural: despite a 40 GB pool, actual consumption is just 33% (13.2 GB). Suite passengers and senior pilots spend most of their time on hotel WiFi — the generous pool is the marketing story; the low actual consumption is what makes the economics exceptional. Pool expansion is free; what matters is actual GB consumed.

4.2 First is the second-strongest premium tier

First delivers $28.05 gross profit / user / month at 67% margin — the second-highest absolute contribution in the ladder. The 25 GB pool with 40% consumption (10 GB actual) keeps COGS at $13.40. The 40% refuel attach adds $1.46 net on top. First's role is to bridge Business to First Suite on the price ladder, and it does so while remaining highly profitable in its own right.

4.3 Business is the default RECOMMENDED tier

Business sits at the broad-market sweet spot: 70% gross margin, $21.86 profit per user, the highest absolute profit among tiers accessible to all crew demographics. It's the "everyone should buy this" tier on the price card. This is why it carries the RECOMMENDED flag in the app.

First is the "ladder rung" tier. Its purpose is to make First Suite look reasonable — but unlike a typical anchor SKU, it's also highly profitable at $28.05/user. Running First costs nothing; it earns well.

4.4 Economy is a volume play

At $3.65 gross profit per top-up, Economy needs 2.5+ purchases per month to compete with even the cheapest subscription tier. A user who buys 2 top-ups in a month delivers $7.30 — less than half of Premium Economy. The strategic role of Economy is twofold: (1) acquisition — a $4.99 try-it price has near-zero friction, and (2) upsell anchor — once users buy 3 top-ups in a month ($14.97), they realize Premium Economy ($17.99 for 10 GB) is a better deal and convert.

4.5 Cockpit is about cash flow, not per-user max profit

At $25.67 monthly-equivalent revenue and $15.82 gross profit, Cockpit underperforms Business ($21.86) and First Suite ($33.03) on a per-user-per-month basis. That's by design. Cockpit's value is $299 collected upfront on day 1 — strong cash flow, zero churn for 12 months, predictable annual revenue stream. The 62% margin is the lowest in the ladder, but the annual prepay is the trade-off.

4.6 The upgrade arithmetic

Every upgrade path has a measurable per-user-per-month profit delta:

Upgrade pathProfit deltaAnnual delta per user
Economy → Premium Economy+$4.23/mo+$51/yr
Premium Economy → Business+$8.50/mo+$102/yr
Business → First+$6.19/mo+$74/yr
Business → First Suite+$11.17/mo+$134/yr
First → First Suite+$4.98/mo+$60/yr

Implication: the strongest in-app upgrade prompt to design is Business → First Suite, skipping First entirely. Marketing should funnel Business users directly to First Suite, not nudge them through First.
