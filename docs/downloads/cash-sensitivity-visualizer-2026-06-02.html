2Failed-eSIM Cost — Corrected Policy

The raw sheet shows $4.00 per failed activation. That figure is wrong for our actual policy. The real blended cost is ~$10.80 per failure.

Sheet shows $4.00 per failure — this was based on a generic refund assumption. Our locked policy is different:
  • Goodwill = extra GB credit (not cash). 2 GB goodwill credit = ~$2.69 supplier cost.
  • User-error failures capped at 3/year per user — beyond that, no goodwill issued.
  • Reissue cost (new eSIM provisioning + support time) = ~$3-5.
  • Estimated refund scenario (genuine carrier failure) = ~$5 credit, not full cash refund.
  • Blended cost per failure ≈ $10.80 (supplier COGS + support time + goodwill GB value + partial refund where applicable)

3Cockpit Annual Cash Advantage — Self-Funding Mechanism

Cockpit users pay $299 upfront annually. We recognize it monthly at $24.92/mo. The timing gap creates real working capital — essentially free cash that reduces how much founders need to inject.

At 100 Cockpit users: $14,950 extra working capital + $598/yr interest

At 1,000 Cockpit users: $149,500 extra working capital + $5,980/yr interest. This is not extra revenue — it's the timing value of collecting cash before the service is delivered.

The self-funding insight: For a bootstrapped business, Cockpit prepay directly reduces founder cash injection need. If you have 100 Cockpit users at launch, you arrive at month 2 with $14,950 of float — that's money already in the bank covering your runway while you wait for the rest of the user base to grow. Push Cockpit aggressively. It's not just a revenue play — it's a cash flow strategy.
