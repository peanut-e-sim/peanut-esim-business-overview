Here's how Peanut eSIM makes money.

We sell crew a monthly data pool. Most crew don't use all of it. The unused gigs are pure margin — we never pay our supplier for them. Layer that across 7 tiers and a 75/20/5 country mix, and the business prints predictable cash.

1The 7-tier ladder

Every tier is anchored to a cabin class. The price ladder mirrors the seat ladder — crew already know it, so it sells itself. First Suite is the anchor at the top; Business is what we recommend most.

2How we actually make profit: breakage

Crew rarely burn their full pool. They pay for 10 GB, use ~4 GB, and the carryover goes one of four places: used on the next trip, used at home in UAE, gifted to family, or expired as pure margin. Every unused gig is margin we never owed our supplier — unless they redeem it intelligently.

The trick: We charged for 10 GB but only paid our supplier for ~4 GB used this month + at most 3 GB carried into next month. The remaining 3 GB? Expires as pure margin. Below: what happens to those 3 carried GB.

What happens to Aiko's 3 GB carry — 4 scenarios

The carryover pool is a flexible balance in our system. Each scenario routes the same 3 GB differently. Locked policy: cancellation = gift-only exit (no cash refunds). UAE redemption and gifting both ship in v1.1–1.2.

SCENARIO A

Use on her next trip

Aiko flies Bangkok in March. We route her 3 GB carry to her travel eSIM. She uses it normally.

  • Our COGS: 3 × $1.34 = $4.02
  • User happy. No "wasted data" feeling.
  • Liability shrinks normally.
Net P&L impact: neutral (paid as planned)
SCENARIO B · UAE

Use at home in UAE

Aiko's du plan runs out at home. She allocates 3 GB carry to a UAE-roaming eSIM (v1.2 feature) and uses it in Dubai.

  • Our COGS: 3 × $1.40 = $4.20 (UAE is Tier-B)
  • Daily engagement habit forms — huge retention win.
  • Eliminates "wasted data" perception entirely.
Net P&L impact: -$0.18 vs A · retention boost worth $30+ LTV
SCENARIO C · GIFT

Gift to her mom in India

Aiko gifts her 3 GB to her mom (v1.1 feature). We route allocation to a new eSIM India profile. Mom installs Peanut to receive it.

  • Our COGS: 3 × $0.85 = $2.55 (India is Tier-A cheap)
  • Mom becomes hot acquisition lead — potential LTV $134.
  • Aiko emotional engagement +++ (helping family).
Net P&L impact: -$1.47 + viral acquisition channel
SCENARIO D · CANCEL

Aiko cancels Peanut entirely

She taps cancel. App prompts: "Want to gift your 3 GB before you go?" Her choice:

  • If she gifts → see Scenario C economics (viral hook + small cost)
  • If she forfeits → 3 GB never delivered = $4.02 pure margin retained
  • No cash refund. No ghost account. Clean books.
Net P&L impact: +$4.02 if forfeited (pure profit)
The full picture: Carryover is not just "data we owe." It's a flexible currency that drives engagement (Scenario B), viral acquisition (C), and clean cancellation economics (D). The "expiry trick" (100% carry with 30-day expiry on premium tiers) + the 3-channel redemption mechanic = the real business model. Premium feels generous. We capture margin anyway.
