## 8. Surprising Findings

1. **Philippine number is lower than expected from perception.** "775 Filipinos" out of ~22,000+ = only ~3.5% hard floor, not the near-dominant group often perceived. They are top-10 but likely not top-3 by count. Filipino over-indexing in community forums may create the impression of higher share.

2. **Egypt is reliably top-5**, consistently appearing across all community-sourced nationality rankings. This Arab representation slot is entirely absent from Balraj's original matrix — adding it above is important.

3. **Crew are 100% DXB-based** — every single Emirates cabin crew member lives in Dubai. This is a significant operational advantage for Peanut: there is one city, one community to seed, no geographic fragmentation. The new 12,000-bed Crew Village at DIP is the single best physical activation point.

4. **Majority of crew are junior (0–4 years, ~59%)** — this cohort is the most valuable for Peanut: highest route variety, highest eSIM spending propensity, most likely to engage with a new brand.

5. **"South Asian" is a more useful label than "Indian" for both crew reality and eSIM marketing.** Sri Lankan, Nepali, and Bangladeshi crew are present but individually invisible in public data. An Indian-language eSIM onboarding flow captures this cluster.
