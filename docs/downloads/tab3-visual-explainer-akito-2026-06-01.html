4The 3 Economy scenarios — PAYG math

Economy is PAYG, not a subscription. Revenue = number of $4.99/GB top-ups the crew buys. The more they fly and use data, the more refuel events we capture. Three scenarios cover the full behavioral range.

6Ladder health — 62–73% margin across all tiers

No tier in the ladder loses money. The range is 62% (Cockpit, by design — annual lock-in) to 73% (Economy PAYG, Refuel). The main strategic risk is geo-mix: Tier-C routes (India, Brazil, South Africa) cost more per GB at wholesale.

9Response playbook — what to focus on

The ladder is healthy. Three moves will keep it that way and improve it further.

1

Negotiate Tier-C wholesale cost

Scenario A shows that a wholesale spike to $2.00/GB drops Premium by $2.77 and Business by more. The blended $1.34/GB depends on only 5% Tier-C geo mix. If Emirates adds routes to India, Brazil, or South Africa, that mix moves — and our cost with it.

Balraj should explore a direct agreement with the wholesale supplier to cap Tier-C rates or secure a volume-based blended rate. Consider a USA/Cruise add-on SKU priced for Tier-C economics as a hedge.

Owner: Balraj — supplier negotiation
2

Push refuel attach via in-app UI

Scenario D shows that doubling refuel attach adds $0.73/user/month to Premium — nearly pure ROI from a UX change. The mechanism: a persistent "You've used 80% of your pool" notification with a one-tap refuel button.

This is Akito's lane. The notification copy, timing, and visual design matter. Crew who see it and tap = $3.65 contribution per event. Crew who churn off because they ran out = $0 + a lost subscriber.

Owner: Akito — app UX + notification copy
3

Push Business → First Suite upgrades

First Suite is the surprise of the model: $33.03/user/mo at 65% margin — the best absolute contribution in the ladder. The upgrade path from Business ($21.86) to First Suite adds $11.17/user/month.

This is a clean in-app marketing moment: "Upgrade to First Suite — 40 GB for just $20 more." The delta in perceived value (2.7× more data) vs. price (1.67× more money) is compelling for crew who travel heavily.

Owner: Akito — upgrade prompt design
