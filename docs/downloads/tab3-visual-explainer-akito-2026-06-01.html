4The 3 Economy scenarios — PAYG math

Economy is PAYG, not a subscription. Revenue = number of $4.99/GB top-ups the crew buys. The more they fly and use data, the more refuel events we capture. Three scenarios cover the full behavioral range.

6The red flags — First and First Suite lose money

This is not a modeling artifact. The math is straightforward: when 60% of a large pool is consumed, the wholesale cost exceeds the retail price. Pilots and FA seniors who actually use their data are net-negative subscribers.

Why we're being this blunt: The in-depth model uses 60% consumption for First/Suite based on the Tab 2 behavioral assumption that higher-tier crew (pro creators, heavy commuters) actually burn data. If that assumption is wrong and real consumption is 30–40%, both tiers recover. But we have to decide: do we believe pilots really use 30–48 GB/month? Until we know, we should not offer these tiers at current pool sizes.

7Refuel — the hidden SKU that stacks on everything

9Response playbook — what to do about this

Three moves. Two are operational (Balraj's lane). One is marketing (Akito's lane). All three need to be addressed before launch or shortly after.

1

Rebalance First & First Suite pools

At 60% consumption, the 50 GB and 80 GB pools are too large for the price. Cutting First to 30 GB and First Suite to 40 GB brings both tiers positive. Alternatively, price increases to ~$54.99 and ~$69.99 achieve the same result without changing the offer.

Neither move is major marketing — crew are used to "pool sizes may change" on eSIM plans. But the decision needs to happen before we go live.

Owner: Balraj — pricing decision
2

Negotiate Tier-C wholesale cost

Scenario A shows that a wholesale spike to $2.00/GB drops Premium by $3.60. The blended $1.34/GB depends on only 5% Tier-C geo mix. If Emirates adds routes to India, Brazil, or South Africa, that mix moves — and our cost with it.

Balraj should explore a direct agreement with the wholesale supplier to cap Tier-C rates or secure a volume-based blended rate rather than per-country billing.

Owner: Balraj — supplier negotiation
3

Push refuel attach via in-app UI

Scenario D shows that doubling refuel attach adds $0.80/user/month to Premium — nearly 100% ROI from a pure UX change. The mechanism: a persistent "You've used 80% of your pool" notification with a one-tap refuel button.

This is Akito's lane. The notification copy, timing, and visual design matter. Crew who see it and tap = $3.65 contribution per event. Crew who churn off because they ran out = $0 + a lost subscriber.

Owner: Akito — app UX + notification copy
Decision required — before launch
