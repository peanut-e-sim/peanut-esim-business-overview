4The 3 Economy scenarios — PAYG math

Economy is PAYG, not a subscription. Revenue = number of $4.99/GB top-ups the crew buys. The more they fly and use data, the more refuel events we capture. Three scenarios cover the full behavioral range.

9Response playbook — what to focus on

The ladder is healthy. Three moves will keep it that way and improve it further.

1

Negotiate Tier-C wholesale cost

At corrected rates, tier convergence means geo-mix is less of a driver — all three tiers are within $0.54/GB of each other. The bigger risk is pool over-use: Suite, First, and Business go net-negative if crew drain 100% of the pool.

Balraj should explore refuel promotion + in-app low-data warnings to reduce pool-exhaustion events. A pool-size audit (are 40 GB / 25 GB / 15 GB the right sizes?) is the highest-ROI model review.

Owner: Balraj — supplier negotiation
2

Push refuel attach via in-app UI

Scenario D shows that doubling refuel attach adds $0.73/user/month to Premium — nearly pure ROI from a UX change. The mechanism: a persistent "You've used 80% of your pool" notification with a one-tap refuel button.

This is Akito's lane. The notification copy, timing, and visual design matter. Crew who see it and tap = $2.73 contribution per event. Crew who churn off because they ran out = $0 + a lost subscriber.

Owner: Akito — app UX + notification copy
3

Push Business → First Suite upgrades

First Suite is the best absolute contribution tier: $20.70/user/mo at 41% margin. The upgrade path from Business ($15.42) to First Suite adds $5.28/user/month.

This is a clean in-app marketing moment: "Upgrade to First Suite — 40 GB for just $20 more." The delta in perceived value (2.7× more data) vs. price (1.67× more money) is compelling for crew who travel heavily.

Owner: Akito — upgrade prompt design
