1. What Tab 2 does and why it matters

Tab 2 answers one question:

Given that a crew member buys a plan, how much data will they actually use — and how often will they refuel?

This single prediction drives every downstream tab. We pay our supplier per GB consumed, not per GB sold. So predicting consumption accurately is the difference between profitable and unprofitable.

The tab has two layers:

LayerWhat it does
Layer 1 — ArchetypesDefines 4 generic user types (Light / Average / Heavy / Churner) with their consumption + refuel attach rates. Conceptual model.
Layer 2 — Per-tier consumptionApplies tier-specific consumption rates that override the archetype weighted average. The actual driver of COGS.

2. The 4 user archetypes

Every paying crew member falls into one of four buckets, regardless of tier:

Archetype% of users% pool consumedRefuel attachMental picture
Light25%30%10%"Subscribed Premium just in case." Pool sits mostly unused. The profit-engine cohort.
Average40%65%30%Default crew. Eats most of pool. Refuels ~1 month in 3.
Heavy30%95%65%Long-haul + connectivity dependent. Almost full consumption, often refuels.
Churner5%10%0%Tries one month, cancels. Single-month pure breakage.

Why crew-skewed (25/40/30/5) instead of general (40/35/20/5)

Crew fly 8-12 layovers/month as their job. They're heavier travelers than vacationers. The bell curve shifts right. We're modeling truck drivers, not weekend tourists.

Confidence: My estimate — the 25/40/30/5 shift is logical but not validated by crew-specific data. Original 40/35/20/5 is grounded in general SaaS benchmarks (RevenueCat, ProfitWell, OpenView).

3. Per-tier consumption — the real driver

After we locked the 1-GB refill UX, the single weighted average from archetypes no longer reflected reality. Friction makes a Premium user behave differently than a First Suite user even when they're both "Average."

So we replaced the single weighted average with per-tier rates:

Confidence: My estimate — these per-tier rates are derived from the friction logic. Real consumption could land 30-70% depending on UX behavior. App analytics months 1-3 post-launch will replace estimates with measured values.

4. The 1-GB refill UX (the key insight)

Users see only 1 GB active at a time. When that GB runs out, they tap "Refill from pool" to get the next GB. The pool is always visible (transparency), but refill requires manual action.

This single design choice produces three compounding effects:

EffectMechanism
Lower consumptionFriction-limited usage drops from ~62.5% to ~40-60% per tier
More breakageUnused pool either carries (deferred breakage) or expires immediately
Pool expansion is freeGoing from 25 GB First to 40 GB First Suite doesn't change COGS — friction still caps usage at ~13 GB. Bigger pool just improves perceived value.

The "pool expansion is free" insight in numbers

TierPoolFriction-capped consumptionPer-GB price
Premium Economy10 GB4 GB (40%)$1.80
Business15 GB6.75 GB (45%)$2.00
First25 GB13.5 GB (54%)$1.60
First Suite ⭐40 GB13.2 GB (33%)$1.25 ← best on ladder

First Suite consumes the same absolute GB as First but with a 60% bigger pool. Same COGS, dramatically better marketing position. That's why First Suite is the anchor product.

5. Breakage column — where profit appears

Breakage = GB the user paid for, but never used. Once it expires (no carryover left), we keep the revenue but never pay our supplier. 100% margin on those GB.

TierPoolConsumedUnusedCarriesImmediate Breakage
Premium Economy10 GB4 GB6 GB3 GB (50%)3 GB ⭐
Business15 GB6.75 GB8.25 GB5.78 GB (70%)2.48 GB
First25 GB13.5 GB11.5 GB11.5 GB (100%)0 GB (deferred)
First Suite40 GB13.2 GB26.8 GB26.8 GB (100%)0 GB (deferred)
Cockpit12 GB7.2 GB4.8 GB4.8 GB (100%)0 GB (deferred)

Why top tiers show ZERO immediate breakage

First, First Suite, and Cockpit all have 100% × 2 month carry. That means month 1 unused rolls forward, doesn't break immediately. Real breakage hits at end of month 2 as carryover ages out.

Tab 10 (Carryover Liability) tracks this aging properly. Tab 2 shows only the monthly snapshot.

8. Ethics — transparency-first breakage

Breakage is a real profit lever. But it's only ethical if the user has every chance to consume what they paid for.

Locked transparency ruleWhy
Pool ALWAYS visibleNever hide what user paid for
Refill button visible at all timesOne tap, no hidden friction
Day-25 reminder push notificationGive them every chance to consume
Carryover shown explicitly: "Y GB will carry, Z GB will expire"Honest disclosure
Breakage we earn from honest forgetfulness is fair revenue. Breakage we earn from hidden mechanics is fraud.

This was Balraj's call (2026-06-01). The locked policy means even though the 1-GB refill UX creates more breakage, every user has full visibility and explicit reminders. They can fully utilize their pool if they want to.
