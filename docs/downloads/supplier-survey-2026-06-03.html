Wholesale Partnership Questionnaire

Pre-call questionnaire

Thanks again for the productive technical call and for moving forward on API access. Ahead of our next conversation, we've put together this short questionnaire so we can come prepared and you can answer at your pace.

The questions are grouped into Pricing, Commercial Model, and Technical. Please fill in what you can — happy to discuss anything that's easier on a call. When done, you can use the Copy responses button at the bottom to paste into a reply email, or print to PDF.

Where it helps, we've noted what we're trying to plan for so the answer can be targeted. If a question doesn't apply or is better discussed live, just write “let's discuss” in the box.

Respondent details

APricing

Confirming the rates that will apply to our account and understanding the broader pricing structure.

A1
Could you confirm that the per-GB rates on the Standard rate sheet you shared will be the rates applied to our account from day one, with no overlay or surcharges at our tier?
A2
For our four highest-traffic destinations — India, Brazil, South Africa, and the USA — could you confirm the exact per-GB rate, and flag any country-specific fees that may apply on top?
A3
Are there any countries on Emirates' route network that your platform does not currently cover? We'll share the full 67-destination list ahead of the call so this can be reviewed in detail.
A4
What customer tiers exist above Standard (e.g. Premium, Wholesale, Strategic Partner), and what are the typical qualifying criteria — volume thresholds, annual commitment, prepay, etc.?
A5
If we commit to a 12-month volume floor, would you be able to lock current rates for that period, or offer improved rates against the commitment? Any guidance on the kind of volume that would unlock either would be helpful.
A6
Are there any platform fees, monthly minimums, activation fees, or per-eSIM provisioning fees in addition to the per-GB rates that we should plan for?
A7
We'd like to propose operating on a prepaid USD balance — we wire $2K–$3K upfront, each order is debited at the per-order rate, and we top up incrementally as the balance draws down. Is this workflow something you can support?
A7a
If a prepaid balance works on your side, could you confirm: no expiry on unused balance, refundable on account closure, and a small minimum top-up (ideally $500 or less)?
A7b
Given a prepaid balance removes AR risk and improves cash-flow predictability on your side, would you be able to offer a discount on the per-GB rate compared with a Net-30 arrangement? If so, roughly what level?
A8
How is FX handled? We're a Delaware LLC paying in USD — are rates USD-quoted, and are there wire-transfer or settlement fees on our end we should plan for?
A9
Our customers (Emirates cabin crew) typically layover for 1–2 days, so the 7-day validity on the standard 1GB Fixed bundle is longer than we need. Two-part question:

(a) Would you be open to creating a 1-day or 2-day 1GB Fixed variant, priced proportionally to the 7-day SKU?

(b) If a new SKU isn't feasible in the short term, what discount could you offer on the existing 1GB/7-day rate to reflect the reduced validity window our customers actually use?

BCommercial model

Understanding how billing, payment terms, and credit work at our scale today and as we grow.

B1
Could you confirm that pay-per-order (one eSIM provisioned = one charge, with no minimums or prepay commitment) is available for our account? If a different default applies, please describe it.
B2
What is the unit of charge — per eSIM activation, per GB consumed, or per plan sold (e.g. a 5GB Europe SKU)?
B3
What are the standard payment terms — billed at time of provisioning, end-of-month invoice, Net 30, or prepay auto-deduct?
B4
If we choose to fund a larger prepaid balance upfront (for example $50K), what minimum prepay earns a discount, and what discount level should we expect?
B5
What is the credit limit / accounts-receivable cap on an account our size today, and how does that scale as we grow toward 5K active users?
B6
When an order fails to provision or activate, are credits issued automatically, or do we need to file a claim each time? If a claim is required, what's the typical turnaround?

CTechnical

Specifications that determine what we build, how the install and top-up flows feel, and how we operate post-launch.

C1
Does your reseller API support same-SIM data top-ups — i.e. adding GB to an existing eSIM profile without requiring re-installation? If not currently available, is this on the roadmap?
C2
Does your platform support eSIM installation via Apple Universal Link, or is QR code currently the only install method?
C3
Is billing based on GB consumed (real-time usage tracking) or GB allocated (the full pool sold to the customer)?
C4
When a customer uses only part of a bundle (e.g. 4GB out of a 10GB pool), what happens to the unused balance — credited back, carried forward, or expired at end of cycle? If it carries forward, at what rate is it billed when used in a later period?
C5
On a single installed eSIM profile, can a customer:
(a) Sequentially purchase new country bundles over time after earlier ones expire (for the lifetime of the eSIM)?
(b) Hold concurrent bundles for different countries with independent expiry?
C6
What is the typical end-to-end provisioning latency — from API call to an eSIM profile being ready to install on the device — in the typical case and at the 99th percentile?
C7
For same-SIM top-ups, what is the typical latency from purchase confirmation to the new GB being usable on the active device?
C8
Could we get sandbox access plus full API documentation (provisioning, usage queries, top-up, refund, and cancel endpoints) this week so engineering can begin building in parallel with the commercial discussion?
C9
Can we pull per-user consumption data via API on a daily basis for our internal COGS dashboard, and how is that data reconciled against your monthly invoice?
C10
Could you share your historical platform uptime, the credit policy for SLA breaches, and the escalation path for a production incident outside business hours? (Our customer base operates 24/7 globally, so out-of-hours coverage matters.)
C11
When our customers install the eSIM, what brand name appears in the device's eSIM profile (e.g. in iPhone Settings)? Can we control that label per SKU to display our own brand?
C12
On a failed activation, what diagnostic information does your API return — specific error codes, root-cause category (device incompatibility, carrier issue, APN misconfiguration)? This directly shapes how our support team handles failed installs.

DAnything else

Anything else we should know — or any questions back to us — before the call?
