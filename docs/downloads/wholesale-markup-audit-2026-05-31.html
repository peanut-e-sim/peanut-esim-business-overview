Wholesale supplier markup audit — the 40% number is a lie

Per-country audit of the current wholesale supplier's actual markup on the 1GB / 1-day pay-per-order SKU across 30 top crew destinations. Three findings emerge: actual markup is ~70% (not the claimed 40%), 12 of 30 destinations are missing from their 1-day catalog, and bypassing them via direct API saves $0.17–$7.59 per activation.

The punchline

Across 18 matched crew destinations, the wholesale supplier's actual markup over their own cost averages 68% (median 70%). Their public "40% margin" framing doesn't match what's in the rate sheet. The cash-impact countries — South Africa, India, UK, Saudi Arabia — show $1.17 to $7.59 in savings per activation if Peanut eSIM contracts direct with the upstream supplier instead.

These are major Emirates crew layover destinations. The 1-day pay-per-order SKU shelf is structurally incomplete, forcing crew users into multi-day or larger-GB bundles that drive up per-trip cost.

Negotiation arsenal — 3 specific asks

  1. Drop markup from ~70% to flat 40% (their public number). Across 30K activations/month at current mix, this saves Peanut ~$15–25K/month. Their own pitch materials state 40% — hold them to it.
  2. Fill the 12 missing 1-day SKUs. Specifically Thailand, Mauritius, France, Germany, Italy, Spain. Without these, every crew layover in these countries costs us a multi-day plan we don't need.
  3. Match direct-supplier rate on top 5 cash-impact countries. South Africa, India, UK, Saudi Arabia, Brazil. If supplier won't go below 40% markup here, we route these countries to a different wholesaler — they keep the easy markets, we cherry-pick the expensive ones.
