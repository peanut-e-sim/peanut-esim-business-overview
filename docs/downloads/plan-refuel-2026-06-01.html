Who it's for

The persona behind this plan

Refuel isn't a standalone plan — it's an add-on that subscribers purchase when they exhaust their monthly pool mid-trip. Example: Priya (Premium Economy) used her 10 GB allocation on a long DXB–SYD layover. She's got 3 days left in Sydney and needs data. One tap → $4.99 → 1 GB topped up instantly. No plan change, no subscription upgrade, no friction. Refuel captures the overflow demand that subscriptions can't serve without raising plan prices.

Refuel is also the only product Economy users interact with — their entire experience is Refuel top-ups without a subscription.

The honest read

Refuel: 55% margin per top-up (old $1.34/GB invalidated; corrected to $2.26/GB, rebuilt 2026-06-02). Not a primary tier — $2.73 per refuel × attach rate × subscribers = hidden upside. Refuel remains the cleanest unit in the model: 100% consumption, no breakage math, one-tap UX. Across 5,000 users at 15% blended attach: ~$2,048/mo pure margin. The key design principle: the Refuel screen must be frictionless — a single "Add 1 GB for $4.99" banner at 80% pool consumption, inline in the app. The UX should never feel like an upsell.
