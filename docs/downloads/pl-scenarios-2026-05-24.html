Unit Economics & P&L Scenarios

Wholesale Supplier Analysis · 9,091 SKUs · CNY→USD @0.139 · Generated 2026-05-24

TL;DR

Break-even lands at just ~6 paying users — fixed costs are that low ($60/mo). At 1,000 users the business generates ~$10,400/mo net profit ($124K annualised) at a ~39% net margin. At 5,000 users that scales to ~$52,000/mo ($624K/yr). The wholesale supplier catalog has excellent wholesale pricing for most of the crew-relevant world — Europe, East Asia, SE Asia, Korea — but South Africa, Philippines, and Mauritius have margin-crushing unit costs that require premium retail pricing. The pay-per-activation model means unactivated subscriber GB is pure retained margin: every user who doesn't fill their pool is free money.

Key Risks & Variables

Decision factors
High

Philippines / S. Africa / Mauritius margins

These 3 destinations are margin-thin or negative at competitive pricing. Only viable at $7–8 retail vs Airalo's $4–6. Strong argument for bundle-only (no PPD) at these destinations.

High

India — no fixed-GB SKU in catalog

Wholesale supplier only offers India as 1GB/day daily-billing. A 7-day trip costs $24.78 wholesale — pricing at $5.99 loses money. Must price India at $7.99+ or avoid India Tier 1 entirely.

Medium

Average GB consumption assumption

Model assumes avg 60% pool utilisation. If crew uses 90%+ routinely, wholesale costs rise ~50% and monthly tier margins compress from ~40% to ~20%. Monitor actual usage tightly post-launch.

Medium

Wholesale 40% markup confirmed; direct not yet secured

Moving to a direct Tier-1 supplier relationship would 3.5× net profit on every plan. This is the highest-leverage single action in the entire roadmap. Priority for Phase 2.

Opportunity

Thailand & Korea: extraordinary wholesale rates

Both destinations have sub-$0.30/GB wholesale cost. Peanut can price well below Airalo ($4) while retaining 85%+ margins. These are loss-leader pricing opportunities to win early users.

Opportunity

Pool carryover = free profit on light users

A T3A user who only uses 3GB of their 10GB pool costs $6.60 in wholesale fees but pays $24. That's $17.40 gross margin ($14.10 net). Encourage subscriptions — discourage PPD for light users.
