Who uses Peanut matters
almost as much as how many.

Same 1,000 users, same supplier costs. If those users are mostly Economy PAYG, monthly revenue is $14,553. If they climb the tier ladder, it becomes $21,476. That 37% gap is driven entirely by which plan they choose — not by data consumption, not by churn. Tier mix is the number one profit lever.

1Layer 1 — User archetypes (consumption behavior)

These four archetypes describe HOW users consume data. Different archetypes have different breakage rates — which affects our margin since we only pay the supplier for data actually used.

Casual Travelers
Non-crew leisure users. Fly 2-4 times per year. Light data use, low loyalty. Acts like an Airalo customer — tries once, may never return.
40% light users 5% churn risk Low frequency
Crew Standard
Base case. Cabin crew flying 15-25 flights per month. Mix of short-haul and long-haul routes. Current model assumption for the majority of users.
25% light / 40% avg / 30% heavy Monthly recurring BASE CASE
Power Crew
Heavy-flier dominance. Long-haul pursers and senior crew on multi-leg pairings. They consume nearly all their pool every month. High refuel attach rate.
15% light / 35% avg / 45% heavy Pool-drainers High refuel
One-and-Done
30% of users disappear after one trip. Happens if we accidentally market to casual vacationers instead of working crew. Kills LTV and growth via referrals.
30% churn rate Bad acquisition AVOID
What these archetypes told us: The archetype mix (consumption behavior) matters less than we initially thought. A Power Crew user consumes more GB but doesn't generate more gross profit per month — their pool empties faster, so they refuel more, but the margin on each refuel is the same 73%. The ARPU is higher but the margin percentage is similar. Tier mix (which plan they pay for) is a far bigger profit lever than consumption behavior.
