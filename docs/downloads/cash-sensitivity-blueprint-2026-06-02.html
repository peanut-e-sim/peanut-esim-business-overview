Section 1 — Refuel Attach Sensitivity (@ 1,000 Users)

Refuel is the $4.99/GB top-up product. At ~55% gross margin it's a strong-margin SKU in the catalog (rebuilt 2026-06-02 at $2.26/GB; old 73% figure used invalidated $1.34/GB). What the "Net Margin Impact" column means

The impact column shows the delta vs the 15% neutral baseline. At 5% attach, we're $273/mo below neutral — Refuel is a drag. At 30%, we're $409/mo above neutral. The range from worst to best case is $682/mo at 1,000 users — that's ~$8K/year swing from this one variable alone (rebuilt 2026-06-02 with corrected rate sheet; old $912/$11K used invalidated $1.34/GB).

What drives attach rate

  • UX visibility: If the "Add 1 GB" button requires 3 taps to find, attach will be low. If it appears prominently when data hits 20% remaining, attach will be high.
  • Tier mix: Economy users are more likely to refuel (they have no pool). Premium/Business users have larger pools and may not run out.
  • Destination: Users in data-hungry Tier-C destinations (Vegas, NYC) burn through their pool faster and refuel more often.

Target: 20–25% attach. Build a persistent low-data warning that surfaces the refuel CTA with one tap. This is the single highest-ROI UX feature in the app from a revenue perspective.

Section 2 — Failed-eSIM Cost (Corrected Policy)

The raw sheet shows $4.00 per failed activation across all failure rates. This figure is based on a generic refund assumption that does not match our locked policy.

WRONG (raw sheet): $4.00 per failure. This implies a cash refund of $4.00 for every activation failure. Our policy is NOT cash refunds — it's GB credit goodwill with a 3/year cap on user-error claims.
CORRECT (locked policy): ~$12.63 blended cost per failure (rebuilt 2026-06-02 with corrected rate sheet; old $10.80 used invalidated $1.34/GB). Breakdown:
  • 2 GB goodwill credit (at $2.26/GB blended, corrected 2026-06-02) = ~$4.52 supplier cost
  • Reissue + support time = ~$3–5 (includes support agent time for manual fix)
  • Partial credit where applicable (genuine carrier failure, not user error) = ~$3–5
  • Blended across genuine vs user-error failures = ~$12.63

Source: Tab 22 rows 26–31. D = C ÷ 2 (average float held during the year: full $299 on day 1, declining to ~$0 by day 365 as service is delivered, average ≈ $149.50). E = D × 4% (money-market rate). Fixed from buggy original sheet.

The Original Sheet Bug and the Fix

Original sheet bug: The Cockpit cash advantage calculation compared the annual revenue total from prepay vs monthly billing. This is meaningless — both produce the same $299/yr in total revenue. The timing value (the ACTUAL advantage) was being measured as the wrong thing.
Fix applied: Column D now = C ÷ 2 (average float advantage — average cash held above what monthly billing would have produced, across the full 12-month period). Column E = D × 4% (interest income on that float at money-market rates). These are the two values that actually matter.

Why D = C/2 and not C

On day 1, we have the full $299 in hand that monthly billing wouldn't have paid yet. By day 365, we've delivered all 12 months of service — the float advantage is $0. The average across the year is half the starting value. For 100 users: $29,900 at day 1, declining to $0 by year end. Average float held = $14,950. Interest at 4% = $598/year. That $598 is real money — it goes directly to founders/company.

Why this matters for a self-funded business

This isn't about $598/yr in interest income — it's about the timing of founder cash injection.

Scenario: You're bootstrapping. You need $50,000 of runway to reach 1,000 users. Monthly billing requires you to raise or inject that $50K upfront. But if you have 100 Cockpit users paying $299/yr on day 1, you arrive at month 1 with $29,900 already in the bank — before your first monthly billing cycle completes. That's 60% of your $50K runway target, delivered by users, not investors.

Scaling up: at 500 Cockpit users at launch, you collect $149,500 on day 1. For most bootstrapped SaaS businesses, that's a full year of runway.

Strategic Recommendation

Push Cockpit aggressively from day one. Position it as the annual commitment plan, not just the pilot-only plan.
  • Current framing: "Cockpit = for pilots and senior crew." This limits the addressable audience.
  • Better framing: "Cockpit = for crew who want to commit annually and save 38% vs Roster Bundle monthly." The $299/yr is cheaper than $24.92 × 12 = $299.04 — but the annual commitment creates the prepay behavior we want.
  • Consider a launch promotion: first 50 Cockpit subscribers get a small bonus (e.g., 2 GB extra at activation) — it's cheap goodwill and accelerates the cash timing advantage.
  • At 200 Cockpit users on launch day: $59,800 collected, $29,900 average float, $1,196/yr in interest. This is runway that didn't require a bridge loan.
