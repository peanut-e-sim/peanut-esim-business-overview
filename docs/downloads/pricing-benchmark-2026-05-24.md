# Peanut eSIM — Competitive Pricing Benchmark

**Captured:** 2026-05-24
**Currency:** All prices USD (EUR @ 1.08, GBP @ 1.27)
**Competitors:** Crew SIM, Airalo, Holafly, Nomad, Saily (NordVPN), Yesim, Ubigi (Transatel)
**Destinations:** 20 (Emirates crew layover cities + key inbound markets)
**Plan profiles:** Light (1GB/7d), Standard (5GB/15d), Heavy (10GB/30d), Unlimited
**Total data points filled:** ~285 / 560 attempted (the rest left blank — see Gaps)

---

## Headline Findings

1. **Crew SIM's "one global rate" is a sales story.** Their math becomes brutal above 1GB. On Japan 5GB usage, Crew SIM costs **$26.71**; Airalo costs **$10.50**. Crew SIM is 2.5x more expensive on the SKU that matters most to crew.
2. **Crew SIM only wins three scenarios:** Turkey (cheapest market at EUR 1.02/GB), pay-as-you-go <500MB usage, and emergency 50MB top-ups in obscure countries.
3. **Holafly's unlimited-only model is a poor fit for crew.** Holafly USA = $74.90 for 30 days unlimited; Airalo 5GB/30d = $14. Crew on 24-72hr layovers virtually never need unlimited.
4. **Mauritius is the most broken market.** Every provider charges 2-3x normal rates. Crew SIM $35.55 for 5GB, Airalo entry $6 (vs $4 elsewhere). Real moat opportunity if Peanut can negotiate wholesale.
5. **Saily and Nomad are the cheapest fixed-bundle competitors** for heavy users. Saily matches Airalo on entry pricing and undercuts on unlimited. Nomad wins on heavy bundles (Japan 10GB-30d $17 vs Airalo $18).

## Key Findings Summary

- **Crew SIM is structurally uncompetitive at 5GB+.** Their per-MB billing is a fairness story but the math punishes anyone who actually uses their phone. This is the single biggest crack in their armor.
- **Holafly is the wrong product for crew.** 24–72 hour layovers don't need unlimited at $30/week. Don't compete with Holafly on price — compete by saying "crew use 2–8 GB per layover, not unlimited."
- **Airalo is the real competitor for switching.** Crew using Airalo today (without crew-specific UX) will be Peanut's easiest converts.
- **Saily is the price floor in fixed bundles.** If NordVPN escalates, Peanut could lose price-only customers. Defense = vertical features, not price war.
- **Yesim's per-day pricing is a wildcard.** Cheap for short stays (€1.63/day Hong Kong) but inconsistent across destinations.
