3What "Burn" Actually Means

Plain-English definition for a self-funded operator — not an investor-grade startup.

Monthly burn = total cash going out, including everything.
COGS (wholesale eSIM cost) + OPEX (tools, admin, salaries, marketing) + one-time spend / 12 (amortized). If you spend $200 on LLC filing and $99 on Apple Developer, your annualized burn for those items is ~$25/month. Add Stripe fees (% of revenue) and you have the full picture.

How to Categorize

  • LLC filing = startup cost (amortized)
  • Registered agent = admin expense
  • Apple / Google = software & subscriptions
  • Domain = software & subscriptions
  • Stripe fees = payment processing
  • Wholesale eSIM cost = COGS (not OPEX)
  • Founder draws = owner distributions
  • Legal consults = professional services
  • Open Mercury business checking account (linked to LLC EIN, not personal SSN)
  • Set up Wave accounting (free) — create chart of accounts with the categories above
  • Log every business expense as it occurs — screenshot + categorize same day
  • Run a 1-hour monthly close: reconcile Wave to bank statement, confirm no unlogged items
  • File Form 1065 (LLC partnership return) annually — Vivek/CPA handles; Balraj provides expense log
  • Upgrade to QuickBooks ($30/mo) when revenue exceeds $50K/yr — not before

6The Honest Read

For a self-funded operator

Don't model burn until you have it. The $11K placeholder created investor optics that aren't real.

The Tab 13 $11K/mo figure makes the model read like a venture-backed startup with a developer team and a marketing budget. That framing created a paper breakeven of ~Month 11-12, as though the business would "lose money" for a year before becoming profitable.

The honest version: Gross = Net at $0 spend. There is no 6-month cash drain to survive. The first sale is profitable. The first month is profitable. The only scenario where this changes is if Balraj or Akito start paying themselves before the business has enough retained earnings to support it.

When real expenses arrive (LLC filing, Apple account), they are small enough to be covered by the first few sales. The $11K row should be deleted from Tab 13 and replaced with actual line items only as they are incurred.

Software stack recommendation: Wave Accounting (free) for Year 1 — covers everything: invoices, expenses, profit & loss, tax reports. Upgrade to QuickBooks Self-Employed ($15/mo) or QuickBooks Simple Start ($30/mo) when annual revenue exceeds $50K. No need for anything more expensive than that.
