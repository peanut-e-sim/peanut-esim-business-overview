Peanut eSIM — Competitor Pricing Research

Researcher sub-agent · 2026-06-01 · For pricing strategy & wholesale (Samurai) negotiation

Seven competitors mapped across nine country/region buckets, with carryover, refuel, and subscription behavior. The market splits into three pricing models: FIXED-DATA (Airalo, Nomad, Saily — buy a bucket, it expires), FLAT-UNLIMITED (Holafly — daily rate, no GB cap), and PAYG / SUBSCRIPTION (Roamless, Crew SIM, Firsty, Saily Ultra). Crew SIM is the only direct vertical competitor and is PAYG, not pool-based. Peanut's pool + carryover model is uncontested in the crew vertical and only loosely shadowed by Saily Ultra ($59.99/mo, 30 GB then throttled).

Strategic read

At the placeholder $13 for 10 GB / 30d with 50% carryover and recurring billing, Peanut Premium Economy undercuts every general-travel competitor on global $/GB and matches Firsty Comfort+ on price while offering 2× the data. Holafly Plans is the only structural peer ($49.90/mo unlimited Global) but at 3.8× the price — leaves clear room for Peanut Business ($25) and First ($50) to sit underneath while still adding margin. Crew SIM's PAYG anchor (~$1.06/GB cheap-country floor) is the only competitor that could undercut Peanut on raw $/GB; the wedge is Peanut's predictability (subscription + carryover) vs. Crew SIM's variability (per-country rate, fluctuating bill).

Negotiation implication for Samurai: the market clears 10 GB Global at $22–24 retail (Saily, Nomad) with wholesale typically 30–40% of retail = $6.60–9.60 wholesale cost on 10 GB as the comparable benchmark. To run Peanut Premium Economy at $13 retail with healthy margin, target wholesale < $5 on 10 GB Global — that's the ask.
