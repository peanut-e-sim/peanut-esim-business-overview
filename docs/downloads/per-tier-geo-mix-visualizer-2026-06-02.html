3The Bombshell

5The Akito Ask — Layover Destination Data

We need 5–10 crew-sourced layover destinations to validate the Tier-A heavy assumption. Not turnarounds — real overnight stays where crew actually use data.

What we need from you

Ask 3–5 Emirates crew contacts: "Which city do you use the most mobile data during a layover?" We need cities with 18–48 hour ground time — not short turnarounds.

  • Mumbai (BOM) — classic Tier-A example, 24–48h layovers, heavily Tier-A priced
  • Bangkok (BKK) — another Tier-A city, popular crew destination
  • London (LHR) — Tier-B, significant layover volume
  • We need USA/Canada examples too — these are Tier-C and drive Maya's poor margin
What counts as a "layover" vs a "turnaround": Emirates DXB-based crew spend 1–3 hours on the ground at turnaround points (e.g., DXB-BOM-DXB same day). That 90-minute ground stop does NOT count — crew stay on the plane or in the terminal and don't activate roaming. A real layover is 18+ hours: crew go to a crew hotel, shower, eat, and use the city. ONLY layovers count for this analysis.
Emirates route context (from route research):
  • 140 destinations across 85 countries total
  • 67 LAYOVER countries only (turnarounds excluded) — these are the relevant ones
  • North America (Tier-C): 3 countries (USA, Canada, Mexico) — all 24–48h long-haul layovers
  • Africa (Tier-A): 22 countries — mostly 24h crew layovers
  • SE Asia (Tier-A): Bangkok, Colombo, Manila, Singapore all high-volume layover cities
