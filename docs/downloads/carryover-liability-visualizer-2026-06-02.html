Carryover Liability —
How Much Data Do We Owe Users?

When a Premium subscriber uses only 4 of their 10 GB, we owe them 3 GB next month. That 3 GB has a real cost — $4.03 at the exact blended rate. Multiply across thousands of users and it becomes a balance sheet liability that investors will ask about.

1The 3 Core Concepts

Carryover liability sits at the intersection of three financial ideas. Understanding all three matters for accounting, pricing, and UX design.

Deferred Revenue

Aiko paid $18.99 for Premium. Until she uses all 10 GB, some of that revenue is technically still "owed" as a service. Accounting treats carried GB as a liability on the balance sheet.

Expired Margin

Data that does NOT carry over is pure margin. If Aiko uses 4 GB and 3 GB expires (at 50% carry), we never paid the supplier for it. That 3 GB = $4.03 of margin we KEEP.

Carryover

Data that DOES carry over is a future cost obligation. If Aiko carries 3 GB to next month and uses it, we pay the supplier $4.03 then. It's a contingent liability until redeemed or expired.

3Aiko's $4.03 Breakdown — 5-Step Walk

Aiko is a Premium Economy subscriber. She uses 4 GB of her 10 GB pool. Here's exactly what happens to the other 6 GB.

Pool: 10 GB
Aiko pays $18.99 for 10 GB of Premium Economy
Consumed: 40% = 4 GB used
Aiko only uses 4 GB this month — she flew one short trip
Unused: 6 GB leftover
6 GB sits in the pool at month-end — not used, not yet paid to supplier
Carry %: 50% → 3 GB carried forward
Premium carries 50% of unused GB. 3 GB goes to next month. 3 GB expires (pure margin for us).
Cost if redeemed: 3 GB × $1.3425 = $4.03
This is our future out-of-pocket cost to the supplier IF Aiko redeems. Aiko already paid us — this $4.03 is contingent.
Critical clarification: $4.03 is our COST from the supplier, not a refund to Aiko. Aiko already paid $18.99. The $4.03 represents future data delivery cost we're committing to when we let that GB carry forward. It's a contingent liability — real cost only if she redeems it.

4The Locked Universal Carry Rule

Decided 2026-06-02. The carryover pool always stays the same, with its original expiry date intact — except one precise exception.

Universal Carry Rule (LOCKED): The carryover pool always retains its original expiry date. The ONLY exception: when upgrading to a plan with a longer expiry window, the existing carry expiry EXPANDS to match the new plan.
ScenarioWhat happens
Downgrade (Premium → Roster)Carry keeps original expiry, sits alongside new pool (no expansion)
Upgrade with longer expiry (Business → First)Carry expiry EXPANDS to match new plan (from 1mo to 2mo)
Lateral moveNo change to carry pool
Cancel + resubscribeCarry expired during gap — gone. No resurrection.

6Balraj's Brilliant Innovations

Three features that turn carryover from a passive liability into an active engagement and acquisition engine.

GIFTING FEATURE

Give Away Carry to Trigger Acquisition

Let users gift their carryover pool (not main pool) to family/colleagues. 3 GB gifted = $4.03 COGS we incur anyway. Recipient potential LTV: $134+. At 2% convert rate: massively positive.

UAE REDEMPTION

Spend Carryover at Home in UAE

Because of same-SIM dynamic allocation, carryover isn't locked to a travel eSIM. Route it to a UAE-compatible eSIM. ~$1.40/GB × 3 GB = $4.20/user net cost. Kills "wasted data" perception entirely.

SAME-SIM ALLOCATION

Dynamic Top-Up (Industry Standard)

One eSIM profile per user, pre-loaded at 0 GB. User taps "load 1 GB" → API call to supplier. We only pay supplier when data is allocated — not at signup. 10K signups = $0 supplier cost.

The three redemption channels combined: next-trip (natural usage) + UAE home (daily engagement + retention) + gifting (viral acquisition). Each channel solves a different problem and they complement each other perfectly.

7First Suite Concentration Risk

First Suite has the largest per-user liability ($35.98) and 100% carry for 2 months. At scale, this becomes a material balance sheet item.

At 100K users with 3% First Suite = 3,000 users × $35.98 = $107,940 floating liability

From Suite alone. Add First (2% × $20.14 × 100K = $40,280) and this becomes a meaningful line item that investors will ask about.

The 2-month cap math for First Suite: At 30-day expiry window (recommendation), 50% of the 26.8 GB unused is expected to be redeemed, and ~50% expires (13.4 GB × $1.3425 = $17.99/user) in expired margin. At 5,000 mature users, that's ~$10,995/mo in expired margin, or roughly $132K/year of free margin from carry expiry alone.

8Cumulative Liability Over 12 Months

At 1,000 users and 20% monthly redemption rate, the carryover balance stabilizes around $12K by month 12. Warning: 20% redemption is likely optimistic — crew tend to hoard for summer trips.

The 20% redemption assumption is probably too aggressive. Based on discussion: 5% (hoarders) puts M12 liability at ~$25K. 20% (current model) puts it at ~$12K. 50% (heavy users) = ~$5K. Crew likely hoard data for summer/long-haul months — real rate probably 5–15%, meaning the model understates liability by 2–3x.
