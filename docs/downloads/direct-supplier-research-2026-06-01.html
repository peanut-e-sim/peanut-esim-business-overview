Direct eSIM Supplier Research

Mapping 14 upstream wholesale eSIM suppliers to replace or supplement our current wholesale supplier relationship. Goal: negotiate from a position of strength with 5–8 verified alternatives and understand true market rates before signing any direct deal. All pricing marked verified/unverified — only confirmed figures used.

1 · Executive Summary

TOP PICKS

Top 3 suppliers to pursue first, based on fit for a travel eSIM startup targeting Emirates crew, needing strong MENA/Asia/Europe coverage, a REST API, and no crippling monthly minimums at launch.

🥇 PRIORITY 1

eSIM Access

Upstream Aggregator

The actual upstream of our current wholesale supplier — going direct cuts out the intermediary markup. No minimum order to start. Published tiered rebate structure (10–20% back at $5K–$50K/month). Already understands the exact plan types we're selling.

Why #1: We know their product works (it's what we're already selling). Going direct removes one full margin layer. Entry cost is $0 MOQ — lowest risk entry of any supplier.

🥈 PRIORITY 2

eSIM Go

Wholesale MVNO Platform

Purpose-built for exactly what Peanut needs: travel eSIM, white-label, consumer app. Strong API (REST, full management portal). Standard tier requires only $1K/month for first 3 months. Transparent public tier structure. TNS Global-backed funding in 2026 = stability. 190+ countries.

Why #2: Most transparent pricing in the market. API quality is the best documented. $1K/month minimum is achievable early. Silver tier at $25K/month aligns with our ~Month 4 revenue projection.

🥉 PRIORITY 3

Telnyx

Telecom API Platform

US-listed, publicly priced infrastructure. eSIM at $0.70 + $2/month per SIM. Data from $0.0125/MB at scale (5GB+ tier). No MOQ. No contracts. Designed for developers — REST API, SDKs for iOS/Android/Node. Used by fintechs as white-label eSIM layer. Strong for US/Europe/Asia.

Why #3: Only major provider with real public pricing. At $12.80/GB (5GB+ tier) it's IoT-grade not travel-grade, but useful as a rate floor benchmark + negotiation leverage with other suppliers.

The core play: Get a rate card from eSIM Access Direct, simultaneously request demos from eSIM Go and Telnyx, then walk back to any current supplier negotiations with three competing quotes. The wholesale market data suggests an additional 20–40% COGS reduction is achievable vs current rates at meaningful volume (10K+ monthly orders). At Peanut's Year-1 projections, even a 20% COGS reduction = ~$80–120K retained annually.

5 · Open Questions — What We Couldn't Find

Items where public sources were insufficient. Must be addressed in direct outreach.

  • eSIM Access direct rate card: Prices are hidden behind account login. We know the rebate structure (10–20%) but not the base rate per country/region. The #1 question to answer in outreach.
  • UAE/MENA wholesale rates across all providers: Most providers confirm "200+ countries" but don't specify UAE/MENA wholesale rates publicly. MobiMatter shows UAE retail at $0.30/GB — implies reasonable wholesale, but unconfirmed.
  • eSIM Go actual per-country rate: Tier structure confirmed publicly, but actual per-GB rates for specific countries (UAE, Japan, UK) are not public. Must be requested in demo.
  • Whether our current wholesale supplier blocks direct eSIM Access access: Some sub-reseller contracts contain exclusivity or no-compete clauses. Need to review our current contract before approaching eSIM Access directly.
  • Telnyx wholesale/reseller rates: Public pricing is IoT-grade ($12.80/GB+). Travel reseller programs likely exist but pricing requires custom quote. Gap between public and wholesale rate unknown.
  • eSIM.tech actual MENA coverage: Local eSIM with phone numbers limited to 25 countries (no MENA listed). Global data coverage unclear for UAE specifically.
  • 1GLOBAL minimum volume requirement: "Enterprise only" implied. Unknown if they have a startup or pre-seed program. Juniper #1 roaming ranking suggests premium pricing.
  • Market rate for direct-wholesale vs aggregator: The simology.io data gives a useful $2.80–$4.50/GB range for direct-wholesale at 10K–100K+ GB/quarter. We need to validate this against actual quotes. This translates to roughly $1.12–$1.80/user/month at our average consumption estimates — meaningful vs current structure.
  • Minimum per-GB rate floor: Telnyx public: $12.80/GB. MobiMatter UAE retail: $0.30/GB (wholesale ~$0.10–0.18/GB implied). This spread is enormous — suggests aggregator/reseller pricing is 10–50x the direct carrier rate. The actual direct wholesale rate for UAE data needs to be negotiated, not assumed.
