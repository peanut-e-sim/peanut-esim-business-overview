Updated 2026-06-01

👤Who it's for

The persona behind this plan

Crew who want connectivity without any commitment — think a cabin crew member doing one short trip to Singapore this month and nothing else. They top up when they land, pay for exactly what they need, and forget about it. Example: Priya, a short-haul FA flying DXB–BKK 3× a month, uses ~2.5 GB total and would rather top up than subscribe. Economy is her gateway into Peanut eSIM.

Monetisation thesis: capture the long tail of non-committers, push UX nudges toward 2-3 topups/session, use Economy as a funnel into Premium Economy subscriptions.

📊Pool vs. consumption

1 GB pool — 40% consumed on average (single top-up model)

0.4 GB used of 1 GB pool = 60% headroom. Unlike subscription tiers, Economy has no carry-forward — unused data expires immediately after the trip. This is intentional: it keeps COGS per top-up very low.

🎛️Sensitivity — what if things change?

Base uses 2.5 topups @ $1.34/GB. Scenarios: +10pp consumption, $2/GB wholesale, 3× topups.

🔍The honest read

Economy: Surprisingly profitable PAYG — IF users top up multiple times. At 1 top-up, you make $4.45 which is fine but small. The magic is the 2.5× realistic scenario: $9.13/user is respectable. UX must be engineered to trigger 2nd and 3rd top-ups within the same month — a "low data" banner at 70% consumed, a seamless one-tap refill screen, and a "3-pack" option ($13.99 for 3 GB) to nudge users toward bulk buying. Without this, Economy under-performs. With it, it's a solid funnel tier.
