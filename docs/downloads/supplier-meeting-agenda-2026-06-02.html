Strategic framing. Samurai is a Tier-3 sub-reseller of eSIM Go with a ~40% markup. We already have the Standard rate sheet. The three answers that matter most: (1) the UAE workaround that lets us actually launch, (2) whether their API exposes eSIM Go's same-SIM top-up (Refuel mechanic), and (3) the migration path to eSIM Go direct if we outgrow them. Everything else is secondary. Do not reveal that we've modeled the 3.5× profit jump from going direct — that's our silent leverage.

1UAE launch block — the launch-blocking question

The Standard rate sheet you shared blocks UAE-resident users. 100% of our launch userbase is Emirates cabin crew based in DXB. Without a clear answer here we cannot ship.

#QuestionWhy we're asking
1.1Which specific plans on the Standard sheet ARE usable by UAE-resident users? List the SKUs, country plans, and the per-GB rate that applies.We need a defined SKU list, not a verbal “we'll figure it out.”
1.2If we ship a beta to 5 Emirates crew (DXB addresses, UAE SIM cards in their phones) on 15 Aug 2026, will the eSIMs install and activate on your platform end-to-end?Launch target is 1 Sept 2026. This is a yes/no go/no-go question.
1.3If your Standard plans can't serve UAE crew, can you re-route us via an eSIM Go plan SKU that does — and at what rate?eSIM Go is your upstream; they have UAE-friendly inventory. If you can't serve us, can they?
1.4Does the UAE block apply only to the eSIM profile residency, or also to the user's data plan when roaming OUT of the UAE? (e.g., Emirates crew on a London layover — do they consume from a UAE-blocked profile?)Need to know if the block is residency-only or session-active too. Changes the workaround completely.
1.5If we go live with the workaround you propose, are there ongoing constraints we need to design around (e.g., user must be physically outside UAE before activating)?UX consequence — if true, we need an onboarding screen warning the user.

2Same-SIM Refuel — the moat question

Refuel ($4.99 retail, $3.65 net margin per event) is one of the 8-feature moats vs Crew SIM. The mechanic only works if your reseller API exposes eSIM Go's same-SIM top-up endpoint.

#QuestionWhy we're asking
2.1Does your reseller API expose eSIM Go's same-SIM data top-up endpoint — or does “adding more GB” require a re-install / new QR code?This single answer determines whether Refuel is a 1-tap UX or a 30-step UX. Crew SIM doesn't have this — it's our moat.
2.2Latency from purchase to GB being usable on the active eSIM profile?UX promise is sub-30 seconds. Anything over 2 minutes kills the feature.
2.3Can a single eSIM profile hold multiple concurrent data pools — subscription pool + refuel pool + carryover pool — with separate expiry rules?This is the architecture under our Tab 20 carryover liability + dynamic allocation model. If your API forces a single pool, we have to redesign.
2.4Per-top-up minimums (500 MB? 1 GB?) or activation fees that change the unit economics on a $4.99 / 1 GB sale?If your minimum is 2 GB, the $4.99 refuel SKU breaks.
2.5If same-SIM top-up isn't exposed today, what's the roadmap and timeline? Is it something you'd build for us if we commit volume?Opens the door to a co-build commitment.

3Path to eSIM Go direct — the existential question

You sit between us and eSIM Go (your upstream). Our model says going direct = 3.5× the net profit at 5K users. Either you close that gap, or you become a stepping stone we eventually leave.

#QuestionWhy we're asking
3.1What customer tier exists ABOVE Standard in your structure — Premium, Volume, Strategic Partner? What are the rate differences and qualification thresholds?If “Standard” is what they call our pricing today, there's by definition something better above it. We want the number.
3.2At what monthly volume (GB consumed or $-spend) do we move from Standard to the next tier? Numbers, not promises.Drives our 24-month rollout planning. We need a concrete “at 1.2K users you're here, at 5K users you're there” map.
3.3Would you introduce us directly to eSIM Go when our volume warrants it, on terms that preserve your value-add as integration partner?This is the relationship-defining question. If yes, you have a long-term partner. If no, we're looking for the exit.
3.4If we choose to migrate to eSIM Go direct in Year 2, what's the contract notice, termination cost, and data/account migration process?Procurement Option C readiness. We need to know the door is unlocked before we walk through it.
3.5Would you consider a co-investment structure: we commit a volume floor, you commit a rate ceiling, both for 12 months?Tests whether they want a transactional or strategic relationship.

5Billing mechanics — consumed vs provisioned, carryover, breakage

This single section determines whether our Tab 20 carryover liability is zero ($0 cost on unused inventory) or material ($132K/yr at 5K users).

#QuestionWhy we're asking
5.1Are we billed on consumed GB (only what the user actually downloads) or provisioned GB (the full pool allocated)?If consumed: carryover is zero-cost to us, expired data = free margin. If provisioned: every unused GB is a cost we eat.
5.2When a user's monthly pool expires unused, who keeps the unconsumed GB — does it auto-credit back to us, or is it gone?Our “~$132K/yr free margin from carry expiry” thesis (Tab 20) depends on this.
5.3If a user carries 3 GB into next month and uses it then, are we billed at original-month rate or current-month rate?If rates change between months, this is material. Affects Tab 20 deferred-revenue accounting.
5.4Granularity of consumption tracking — nearest MB, KB, or rounded up to whole GBs?At 100K users, rounding-up adds real cost.
5.5Billing reconciliation cycle (monthly invoice, real-time stream)? Can we pull usage data via API for our COGS dashboard?Needed for real-time P&L. Without it, COGS is always a lagging indicator.

6Failed-eSIM economics

6.4Do you offer a pre-approved goodwill GB credit pool we can draw from for customer-facing refunds, or is every refund a separate request?Our customer-facing policy is “GB credit, not cash refund.” Easier if we have a pool to draw from.
6.5Apple Universal Link installation flow — is your platform compatible? What's the failure rate vs traditional QR code?Our roadmap commits to Apple Universal Link (no Carrier Entitlement). If your platform doesn't support it, this is a launch blocker.

!Strategic tone notes

How to run the conversation, not just what to ask.

  • Lead with the UAE question (Section 1). If they can't serve UAE-resident crew, every other answer is academic. Frame it bluntly: “The Standard sheet you sent blocks UAE users; my entire launch userbase is UAE-resident. Help us solve this first.”
  • Do not reveal the eSIM Go direct math. Don't mention that we've modeled the 3.5× profit jump from going direct, and don't mention the “Option C” in our procurement analysis. If they sense the threat, they harden — we want them open and competitive.
  • Frame trajectory, not current scale. Open by acknowledging we're small today (~50 users) but modeling 5,000 by M24 and Etihad/JAL/ANA in Y2. Suppliers commit better terms for a trajectory than for a snapshot.
  • Three answers matter most. (1) UAE workaround, (2) same-SIM Refuel API, (3) introduction path to eSIM Go direct. Get those even if everything else is “we'll get back to you.”
  • Listen for what they don't volunteer. MFN, exclusivity, termination cost, and 12-month rate locks won't come up unless asked. Their silence on these IS the leverage we identify.
  • Take notes by section number. Section 1 answers go on page 1, etc. Send a written summary email same day — that's how verbal commitments become contractual.
  • End on the calendar. Don't leave without a date for the next conversation. “Let me get my engineer in our next call to verify the API specifics — can we do Thursday?”
