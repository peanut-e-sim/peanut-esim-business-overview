Goal of this meeting

What we're doing here. Three things to walk out with: (1) actual per-GB rates per country we'll be billed at, (2) commercial model confirmed in writing (we want pay-per-order, no minimums, no prepay commitment), (3) technical answers we need to build against (refuel API, Apple Universal Link, billing model, SLA). We are NOT asking permission — we're confirming terms. Frame as a partner conversation between two businesses, not a sales pitch from us.

Opening frame — what to lead with (60 seconds)

This is not a question. It's a positioning statement Balraj delivers at the start of the meeting. The goal is to anchor Samurai's perception of who we are, so the pricing conversation that follows happens on better ground.

“Thanks for getting on the call. Quick context before we dig in: we're Spicy Kiwi LLC — Delaware C-corp, brand is Peanut eSIM. Our wedge is Emirates cabin crew — we launch beta with 5 crew on 15 August, public on 1 September. We've modeled this out 24 months in detail and we're self-funded, so we're not in a hurry to take bad terms.

What we want from this meeting is straightforward: confirm the actual rates we'll be billed at per country, confirm the commercial model (we're assuming pay-per-order, no minimums), and walk through the technical specs we need to build against. If those line up, we move fast. If not, we figure out the gap together.”

Why this framing: establishes we're a real entity, not a hobbyist; signals we're organized (24-month model); signals we have options (self-funded); puts pay-per-order on the table as our assumption rather than asking permission. Sets the meeting on equal footing.

2Pricing — the actual numbers we'll be billed at

We have the Standard rate sheet but need to confirm it applies to us as a Delaware LLC, validate the specific countries that drive our P&L, and probe whether better tiers are available.

#QuestionWhy we're asking
2.1Confirm: the per-GB rates on the Standard rate sheet you shared ARE the rates we'll be billed at from day one. There's no overlay, no markup at our account tier. Yes or no?Locks the baseline. We've modeled $1.34/GB blended off this sheet — if there's a hidden markup, every tier's margin shifts.
2.2For our four highest-traffic destinations, please confirm the exact per-GB rate from the sheet: India, Brazil, South Africa, USA. Are there any surcharges or country-specific fees we don't see on the public sheet?These four drive our Tier-C exposure. 2.3Are there any countries in Emirates' route network that you do NOT support, that we'd need to plug from elsewhere? (We'll send the 67-destination list before the meeting.)Unsupported country = broken UX for the crew member on that layover. We need to know the gaps.
2.4The sheet is labeled “Standard.” What tiers exist above Standard — Premium, Wholesale, Strategic Partner — and what does it take to qualify? Volume threshold? Annual commitment? Prepay?Reveals the discount staircase ahead of us. We don't need it today but we want it on the table.
2.5If we commit to a 12-month volume floor — say, X GB/month or $Y-spend/month — will you lock the current rates in writing for 12 months, or improve on them?Two wins in one ask: (a) rate lock removes model risk; (b) tests their appetite for commitment-for-discount trade.
2.6Are there platform fees, monthly minimums, activation fees, or per-eSIM provisioning fees layered on top of the per-GB rates we should know about?Common hidden cost. If the answer is anything but “no”, our COGS model needs an OPEX line we haven't modeled yet.
2.7How do you handle FX? We're a Delaware LLC paying in USD; are your rates USD-quoted, and are there wire transfer or settlement fees on our end?For a 5K-user business, FX fees and wire charges add up. Better known now than in invoice month 1.

Even a partial reduction (say, 30%) is meaningful at scale. This is the primary commercial ask we walk into the meeting wanting.

3Commercial model — pay-per-order confirmation

From Tab 15 (Procurement Options) we're anchoring on Option A: pay-per-order. We want explicit confirmation, and we want to know what alternatives exist (prepay credit, bulk commitment) and whether they unlock better economics.

#QuestionWhy we're asking
3.1Confirm in writing: we operate on pay-per-order — one eSIM provisioned, one charge to us. No prepay balance required, no monthly commitment, no order minimums. Correct?This single sentence locks the entire commercial baseline. Force a yes/no, and put the confirmation in their follow-up email.
3.2What's the unit of charge — per eSIM activation, per GB consumed, or per “plan” sold (e.g. a 5 GB Europe SKU)?Drives how our checkout flow maps to their API. Per-GB-consumed is cleanest; per-plan forces us into their SKU shape.
3.3Payment terms: when are we billed — at time of provisioning, end-of-month invoice, Net 30, prepay auto-deduct? What's the standard?Affects working capital. Net 30 + monthly billing is friendliest to a self-funded launch.
3.4If we choose to top up a prepay balance (e.g., $50K credit upfront, you deduct as we activate), do we get a discount vs pay-per-order? What's the minimum prepay that earns a discount, and how much?We have Cockpit annual prepay revenue coming in — we can fund a prepay balance directly. Worth testing.
3.5What's the credit limit / accounts-receivable cap at our scale today, and what does it become at 5K users?If they cap us at $5K of unpaid balance, we can't scale through any aggressive acquisition month without prepaying. Better known up front.
3.6Refunds: if an order fails to provision or activate, do you auto-credit us, or do we file a claim each time?Tab 22 models ~$10.80/failure blended. If we have to chase every credit, that number grows.

4Technical specs to build against (the engineer's checklist)

API keys are agreed. These are the technical questions whose answers determine what we build, how we build it, and where the UX edges are.

#QuestionWhy we're asking
4.1Same-SIM Refuel API. Does your reseller API expose eSIM Go's same-SIM data top-up endpoint — user buys 1 GB and it's added to the existing eSIM profile, no re-install? Yes / no / roadmap.This is THE moat question. Refuel is $4.99 retail, $3.65 net margin per event. Crew SIM doesn't have it. Without this endpoint, our biggest differentiator becomes a re-install flow that nobody uses.
4.2Apple Universal Link install. Does your platform support eSIM installation via Apple Universal Link (no QR scan required), or only the traditional QR code flow?Our roadmap commits to Universal Link for the install UX (no Carrier Entitlement needed). If they're QR-only, we need to plan for a worse install conversion rate.
4.3Billing model. Are we billed on GB consumed (real-time tracking of what the user actually downloads) or GB allocated (the full pool we sold to the customer)?Single answer determines whether Tab 20 carryover liability is $0 (consumed) or material (~$132K/yr at 5K users with allocated).
4.4Carryover. When a user uses only 4 GB of a 10 GB pool, what happens to the unused 6 GB at end of cycle — do we get credited, does it carry forward, does it expire? And if it carries, at what rate are we billed when used next month?Depends on 4.3 above. Determines how we account for it in Tab 20 deferred-revenue.
4.5Multiple concurrent pools per eSIM. Can a single eSIM profile hold a subscription pool + refuel top-up pool + carryover pool with independent expiry rules per pool?Our Tab 20 dynamic allocation model assumes yes. If no, we redesign the UX (and lose the carryover-as-feature narrative).
4.6Provisioning latency. What's the typical end-to-end time from API call to eSIM profile ready-to-install on a device? Typical case + 99th percentile?Drives the “signup → activated” UX promise. Anything > 60 seconds breaks our onboarding flow.
4.7Top-up latency. For a same-SIM refuel: from purchase confirmation to GB usable on the active device? We need < 30 seconds for the UX promise.Refuel is sold as an instant in-airport top-up. 2 minutes kills the use case.
4.8Sandbox & documentation. Can we have sandbox access + full API docs (provisioning, usage-query, top-up, refund, cancel endpoints) THIS week?Engineering can't wait for a contract signature. We need to start building this week.
4.9Usage tracking + billing reconciliation. Can we pull per-user consumption data via API daily for our own COGS dashboard, and how do we reconcile that against your monthly invoice?Without real-time usage data, COGS in our P&L is always lagging. Need to plan the dashboard against your data shape.
4.10Uptime SLA + incident escalation. What's your historical platform uptime, what credit do we get on a breach, and what's the escalation path for a production incident at 03:00 GMT (Emirates crew run 24/7 globally)?Emirates crew are airborne or in unfamiliar cities when things break. We can't tell them “file a ticket.” Need a real escalation contact and credit policy.
4.11White-labeling. When our crew installs the eSIM, what brand shows in the device's eSIM profile name — ours, yours, or eSIM Go's? Can we control that label per SKU?Brand visibility matters for trust. Crew should see “Peanut” in their iPhone Settings, not “Samurai” or “eSIM Go.”
4.12Failed-eSIM diagnostics. When an activation fails, what diagnostic info does your API return — specific error codes, root-cause category (device incompatible, carrier issue, APN misconfig)? We need this to power our AI Support agent.Tab 22 says 70% of failures are preventable with UX. We can't prevent what we can't classify.

!How to run the conversation

Tone, sequencing, what to reveal, what to keep silent.

  • Open with the framing card above. Sets the relationship as partner-to-partner. They should hear “Delaware LLC” and “self-funded” in the first 30 seconds — both signal we're not a desperate hobbyist.
  • Lead the pricing block with a confirmation. Question 2.1 forces them to confirm the Standard sheet is what we pay. Then sub-questions become natural.
  • Pay-per-order: state it, don't ask. Question 3.1 is phrased as “confirm in writing” — we assume it's pay-per-order, they push back if it's not. This is a power move; if you ask “what's your model?” they pitch their preferred (probably prepay).
  • Do not reveal: the 3.5× profit math going direct to eSIM Go, the $0 actual OPEX, the 24-month user projections beyond what you stated in framing. If they ask, the answer is “we're modeled for thousands, growing fast — happy to share more if we move forward.”
  • Listen for what they don't volunteer. Hidden fees, MFN clauses, termination terms, white-labeling restrictions. Silence on these = the leverage point we name in the follow-up.
  • End on a calendar commitment. Don't leave without a date for the next conversation. “Let's get our engineer talking to yours Thursday to walk through the API specifics — can we lock that?”
  • Send a written summary same day. Restate every confirmation they made in writing — that's how verbal commitments become contractual. Subject line: “Peanut eSIM — recap of today's call + open items.”
