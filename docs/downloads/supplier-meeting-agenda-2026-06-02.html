1Baseline — what are we paying today?

We already have the Standard rate sheet they shared (eSIM Go format). These questions probe what's NOT on that sheet.

#QuestionWhy we're asking
1.1The rate sheet you shared is labelled “Standard.” What other rate tiers exist above this — Premium, Volume, Strategic, Direct? What does it take to qualify for each?“Standard” usually implies better rates exist for larger customers. We want to know the ceiling, not just the floor.
1.2The shared sheet blocks UAE-resident users. What's the workaround for serving Emirates crew based in DXB? Is there a UAE-specific rate or an alternate plan?100% of our launch userbase is UAE-resident. If the published rate doesn't apply to them, we need the actual rate that does.
1.3Have any rates on the shared sheet changed since you sent it? How do we get notified of future rate-sheet updates?We've baked the rate-sheet numbers into our 24-month financial model. Surprise increases would break that.
1.4Are there fixed monthly platform / minimum-commitment / activation fees on top of the per-GB rates on the sheet?Common to find hidden fees that don't show up on the published per-country rate sheet.
1.5What account tier are we currently on, and what triggers a move to a better tier (volume, time, commitment)?Our 24-month rollout needs a concrete “when do we earn a better deal” answer.
1.6What are your standard payment terms — Net 30, prepay, on-demand?Affects working capital model and our Cockpit-prepay float math.
1.7Is the rate on the sheet the consumed-GB rate, or provisioned-GB? Where does breakage go — back to us or kept by you?Core economic question. Consumed-GB billing makes our carryover liability zero cost; provisioned makes it real.

2Volume discount triggers

When do we earn a better rate? What does the staircase look like?

#QuestionWhy we're asking
2.1What's your published volume-discount schedule? At what monthly GB or $-spend do we move tier?Need this number to know exactly when to come back and renegotiate.
2.2If we commit to X GB/month for 12 months, what discount can you offer?Tests appetite for volume commitment vs pay-as-you-go.
2.3Do you offer prepay discounts (e.g., 6/12-month bulk credits)?Our Cockpit annual prepay can fund inventory prepay if the discount is meaningful.
2.4At our current ~50 active users, are there ANY pricing improvements available, or do we need to hit real volume first?Sets honest expectation. If “no” — we grow first, not negotiate now.
2.5If we reach 1,500 / 5,000 / 10,000 active users in 12–24 months, what tier would that put us in?Anchors a future trajectory and creates a target for them to compete for.

3Cost model & billing mechanics

The plumbing — how money flows between us, what happens to unused inventory, how partial GBs are tracked.

#QuestionWhy we're asking
3.1When a user's data pool carries over to next month, are we billed for carried-over GB at original-month rate or current-month rate?Affects how we model deferred-revenue cost on the balance sheet (Tab 20).
3.2Does an unused pool that expires after our policy window cost us anything?Our “free margin from carry expiry” thesis (~$132K/yr at 5K users) depends on this answer.
3.3How is partial-GB consumption tracked and billed — to the nearest MB, or rounded up to whole GBs?Material for high-volume Tier-C exposure.
3.4Are there any per-activation fees on top of the per-GB rate?Common hidden cost — verify before signing anything.
3.5What's the billing reconciliation cycle, and can we get usage data via API daily/hourly?Needed for real-time COGS in our P&L.

4Geographic coverage & Tier-C exposure

The single biggest cost-side risk in our model. If Tier-C mix shifts, every tier compresses.

#QuestionWhy we're asking
4.1What countries do you classify as Tier-C (premium-cost destinations)? Can we get the full list?We assume 5% Tier-C in our model. Need the actual country list to verify against Emirates' 67 destinations.
4.2Specifically: per-GB rate in India, Brazil, South Africa, and the United States?Our highest-exposure markets. The $2.00/GB stress scenario assumes Tier-C drift.
4.3If our geo mix shifts to 15% Tier-C, what happens to our blended rate?Supplier-side test of our internal sensitivity model.
4.4Do you have any way to cap or hedge Tier-C cost exposure (e.g., a fixed-blend offering)?Would significantly de-risk margin — one of three procurement options we're modeling.
4.5If we launch a USA-focused or Cruise-focused SKU, can you offer a flat per-GB rate?Direct enablement of the geo-risk add-on SKU we have planned.

5Failed activations & support SLA

Failed eSIMs cost us ~$10.80 each in the model. Who eats what?

#QuestionWhy we're asking
5.1What's your refund / credit policy when a user's eSIM fails to activate?We blended-cost this at $10.80/failure. Align expectations.
5.2If failure is due to device incompatibility (not user error), do we get credited?30% of failures in our model. Need to know whether we eat the cost or pass it through.
5.3What's your support SLA — initial response time, resolution time, escalation path?For our customer-facing AI Support feature we need 1st-response SLAs we can promise users.
5.4Is there a goodwill GB allowance for failed activations, or is each one a separate transaction?Affects how we structure our “credit, not refund” customer policy.
5.5What's the typical activation-failure rate you see across your customer base?Calibrates whether our 5–10% assumption is reasonable.

6Same-SIM dynamic allocation (Refuel)

Refuel is a major margin driver ($3.65 net per event). The whole feature depends on supplier-side support.

#QuestionWhy we're asking
6.1Does your platform support same-SIM data top-up — user buys 1 GB and it appears in the existing eSIM profile without re-installation?Our Refuel feature ($3.65/event margin) depends on this.
6.2What's the latency between purchase and the new GB being usable on the device?UX critical — we need to deliver “tap → refuel ready” in under 30 seconds.
6.3Are there any per-top-up fees or minimum increments (e.g., 500 MB minimum)?Affects whether we can sell 1 GB top-ups profitably.
6.4Can a single eSIM profile hold multiple concurrent data pools (e.g., subscription + refuel)?Architecturally important for our Tab 20 dynamic allocation model.
6.5Is there a maximum number of top-ups per eSIM per month/year?Affects how aggressively we can push refuel attach.

8Multi-airline expansion (Y2–Y3)

8.2Can we operate multiple branded products (e.g., one for Emirates crew, one for JAL crew) under the same account?Affects how we structure brand portfolio expansion.
8.3Do you have direct relationships with JP / SG / HK / AU / QA carriers we'd benefit from referencing?Multi-airline expansion targets these countries — supplier-side network matters.
8.4What's your view on us building a regional pricing strategy (different retail per airline market)?Supplier-side opinion is useful even if their answer is “do what you want.”

9Strategic / contractual

The clauses that matter at scale. Most won't be volunteered — we ask explicitly.

#QuestionWhy we're asking
9.1Do you offer exclusivity clauses, and what would those cost us / earn us?Future leverage point.
9.2Is there an MFN (most-favored-nation) clause — same rates as your best customer at our volume?Standard wholesale clause. Worth asking.
9.3If we wanted to switch to a direct wholesale provider in Year 2, what's the contract notice period and termination cost?Critical for our procurement Option C path.
9.4Can we get a written commitment that pricing is locked for the next 12 months?Reduces model risk on our 24-month rollout.
9.5Are you open to a co-marketing arrangement (e.g., we mention you as our infrastructure partner)?Non-cash value worth quantifying if it's on offer.

!Strategic tone notes

How to run the conversation, not just what to ask.

  • Frame trajectory, not current scale. Open by acknowledging we're small today (~50 users) but modeling 5,000 by M24 and 100K longer-term. Suppliers compete for trajectory, not snapshots.
  • Do not reveal Option C. Our $5K/mo direct-wholesale alternative is silent leverage. If they sense it, our negotiating position evaporates.
  • The two answers that matter most: (a) when do we earn a real discount tier, (b) is the billing model consumed-GB or provisioned-GB. Get those even if you walk out with nothing else.
  • Listen for what they don't volunteer. Exclusivity, MFN, and termination terms usually don't come up unless explicitly asked. Their absence is the leverage point.
  • Anchor on the future, close on the present. Spend 70% of the conversation on 5K/10K/100K-user economics, 30% on what changes today. The first conversation rarely produces a new price — it produces a relationship that earns one.
  • Take notes by section number. Section 1 answers go on page 1, etc. Send a written follow-up the same day summarizing what you heard — that's how verbal commitments become contractual.
