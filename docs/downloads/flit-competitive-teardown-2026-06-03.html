The structural disadvantage Peanut cannot ignore Flit is a registered Korean MVNO with direct bilateral agreements (KDDI, China Mobile). Their wholesale cost is roughly 1/3 of Peanut's Tier-3 reseller cost. They can price at $2.45/GB and still earn 70%+ gross margin. Peanut at the same retail would lose money. The wedge cannot be price — it must be feature.

Three pricing implications for Peanut

A

Do not try to price-match Flit's $49/year

At Peanut's $2.26/GB blended wholesale cost, matching Flit's $2.45/GB retail leaves only ~8% gross margin before Stripe, refunds, support, and fraud. The Operator-advisor's xlsx model shows this scenario goes negative at low trip frequency. Peanut's pricing floor on a 20GB-equivalent plan should be ~$79–89/year, not $49. Differentiate on roster awareness + layover intel + English UI, not on $/GB.

B

Annual model is a wedge Peanut should consider for crew specifically

Flit's $49/year annual single-purchase model gives them upfront working capital and zero monthly churn. Peanut's planned monthly subscription has the opposite cashflow profile. For the crew vertical specifically, consider mirroring an annual tier (e.g. "Crew Annual" at $89/yr for 30GB) alongside the monthly subscription. Annual buyers self-select for high engagement and don't need to be re-acquired every month.

C

Match or beat the 1GB free trial — but in English

Flit's Emirates waitlist offer is "1GB free trial." Peanut should run an exactly equivalent offer ("First 2GB free for Emirates crew — English app, USD pricing") and seed it through the same channels Flit cannot reach (English-speaking crew WhatsApp groups, EK Facebook pages). The offer is symmetric; the channel access is not. This is the cheapest competitive move available.

What to learn from Flit

  • Peer recommendation is the acquisition channel. Every Flit crew review cites a colleague recommendation. Build referral mechanics before launch, not after.
  • Airline email verification = trust moat. Crew value the "crew-only" badge. Gate the full experience behind airline email from day 1.
  • eSIM device swap is a real crew pain point. Build this feature. Crew upgrade phones and don't want to re-buy eSIMs.
  • Priority support for crew is a differentiator. Flit explicitly advertises this. Peanut's AI support + crew-priority queue is a response to this pain.
  • Unused data → points refund. Smart mechanic for crew who leave layovers early. Reduces price anxiety.
  • "1GB free trial" as entry offer. Flit is using this exact offer for Emirates waitlist. Peanut should match or beat it as a launch acquisition offer.
  • The crew segment is real and fast-growing. Flit's 4,580 in 7 months validates the hypothesis. This is not a niche — it's a measurable market.

What to avoid

  • Korean-only interface. Emirates crew are 55+ nationalities. English is non-negotiable.
  • No schedule integration. Flit requires manual country/duration selection. Peanut's roster upload removes this friction entirely.
  • No layover content. Data-only utility. Peanut's recs + intel create lock-in that Flit has no answer to.
  • No community. Flit is transactional. Peanut's crew recs and points ecosystem create switching costs.

3 Specific 30-Day Moves
▾
Time-sensitive Flit has the Emirates waitlist open but no English product. This window is estimated at 6–12 months before they can ship English localization. These three moves are sequenced to exploit that gap.
1

Seed Emirates crew networks before Flit ships an English app

Flit's Emirates waitlist uses only 2 English sentences and links to a Korean-language page. Their English product does not exist yet. Target Emirates cabin crew WhatsApp groups, Facebook groups, and crew forums with a counter-offer: "First eSIM free" or "1GB free trial — English app, USD pricing, no Korean required." Goal: establish Peanut as the English-language crew eSIM of choice at DXB before Flit completes localization. Time window: approximately 6–12 months.

2

Lock in "world's first English crew eSIM" positioning across all public assets

Flit has claimed "세계 최초 승무원 전용 eSIM" (world's first crew-only eSIM) in Korean since August 2025. Peanut can legitimately claim "world's first crew eSIM built in English" or "world's first Emirates crew eSIM." Add this language to the App Store description, landing page hero, and all pitch deck slides this week. Once Flit ships English, this window closes. Don't let them claim it retroactively.

3

Build the Flit comparison page as a sales tool

Flit's SKYCREW comparison table is a template Peanut should replicate and invert. Create a comparison page (Peanut vs Flit, not indexed publicly initially) with: English app (Peanut: yes, Flit: no), USD pricing (yes/no), roster-aware plans (yes/no), layover intel (yes/no), multi-airline non-Korean support (yes/no). Deploy this as a direct response whenever crew mention Flit in any forum, and as a PDF attachment for crew group outreach. Flit's Korean-language barrier is the single most concrete competitive advantage to exploit.
