# CrewLink eSIM — Plans & Refund Policy
### A complete guide to how our plans work, optimized for crew who live in the air

---

## What is CrewLink?

CrewLink is a travel eSIM service built specifically for airline crew. Where apps like Airalo and Holafly are designed for tourists who visit one country and go home, CrewLink is built for professionals who might land in Bangkok on Tuesday, Dubai on Wednesday, and Frankfurt on Friday — all in a single working week.

The problem CrewLink solves is simple: every other eSIM app requires you to plan ahead, purchase per country, and manually install packages before each trip. For a crew member operating off a monthly roster, that's an hour of admin every four weeks. CrewLink's answer is the Roster Bundle — upload a screenshot of your monthly schedule from Crew Pulse, and the app automatically identifies every destination, bundles the right data packages together, and gives you a single 15–20% discount for buying in bulk. One tap. Done.

Unlike Crew SIM, our closest competitor, CrewLink doesn't require a carrier contract. Unlike Airalo, we're not a marketplace of random operators — we curate and verify every provider. And unlike Holafly, we don't sell unlimited plans on throttled networks. Every plan is honest about what you get.

## The 7 Plans, Explained Through Cabin Classes

We named our plans after airline cabin classes because crew understand them instantly. Your plan tier is your travel style. From Economy to Cockpit, there's a plan that fits exactly how you fly.

### Economy — One Country. One Tap.

Economy is the entry point. It's designed for a crew member who has a single layover in a country they don't visit often — say, a one-off Melbourne turn-around, or a month where you only fly one long-haul destination outside your usual network.

Pricing ranges from $3 to $7 depending on the country, and you get 1 GB of data with a 3-day validity window from the moment you first use the eSIM. That's enough for maps, messaging, and light browsing during a 24-hour layover.

The key thing to understand about Economy is that it doesn't renew. You buy it when you need it. When it runs out or expires, you top up with a Refuel add-on (more on that below). It's a pay-as-you-go approach for crew who don't want a subscription.

**Best for:** Crew with 1–2 international destinations per month, or anyone who travels infrequently and doesn't want ongoing costs.

---

### Roster Bundle — Upload Your Roster. Skip the Planning.

This is the reason CrewLink exists. The Roster Bundle is CrewLink's signature feature and the single biggest differentiator from every other eSIM service in the market.

Here's how it works: at the start of each month, a crew member gets their schedule from Crew Pulse — Emirates' crew scheduling app. That schedule is a monthly calendar grid showing every pairing: DXB–LHR–DXB, DXB–SIN–BKK–DXB, and so on. The crew member takes a screenshot and uploads it directly into CrewLink.

The app reads the screenshot using OCR. It identifies every destination country. It builds a custom data bundle covering every country in the schedule — pre-configured, pre-installed, ready before you even board the first flight. And because you're buying everything together, you automatically get 15–20% off versus purchasing each country individually through Economy.

For a typical Emirates crew member flying 4–5 destinations per month, that discount adds up to roughly $8–12 per month — or nearly $100 per year in savings, automatically, with no manual work.

Roster Bundle pricing varies by schedule but typically lands between $22 and $55 depending on destinations and data allocation.

**Best for:** Active crew flying 3 or more destinations per month who want the smartest, most automated option.

### Premium Economy — Stepped Up.

Premium Economy is CrewLink's first monthly subscription tier, at $24 per month.

You get a 10 GB data pool to share across any countries CrewLink covers. Fifty percent of your unused data carries over to the next month — so if you only used 6 GB this month, 2 GB rolls forward to June. That carryover feature makes Premium Economy much more forgiving than prepaid options: light months subsidize heavy months.

Think about the crew member who flies domestically within the UAE most weeks but has one or two international turns per month. They don't need First Class data speeds; they need something reliable, portable, and easy. Premium Economy is that product.

**Monthly cost:** $24/month
**Data:** 10 GB pool
**Carryover:** 50% of unused data

**Best for:** Crew who fly regularly internationally but have mostly light data needs — messaging, maps, occasional social.

---

### Business — The Crew's Class.

Business is the most popular subscription tier among working crew. At $34 per month, it gives you 15 GB of pooled data, with 75% carryover. That means if you use 10 GB in April, 3.75 GB of your remaining 5 GB rolls into May.

Business class is designed for the crew member who uses their layover productively — hotel room emails, a video call with family back home, streaming a Netflix episode to unwind. These are the crew members who need more than messaging but don't need unlimited.

At $34 per month, Business costs about $1.13 per day of data coverage. Compare that to buying a local SIM at each destination, dealing with SIM slots, finding the right carrier, and losing data credits when you leave — Business is simpler, more reliable, and in most markets, cheaper.

**Monthly cost:** $34/month
**Data:** 15 GB pool
**Carryover:** 75% of unused data

**Best for:** Working crew who video call, do social media, stream occasionally, and need a dependable month-to-month option.

---

### First — Stream Like You're Home.

First Class is for crew who treat their layover like a mini-vacation. At $44 per month, you get 20 GB of pooled data with 100% carryover — every unused gigabyte rolls forward indefinitely (up to the plan maximum).

The 100% carryover changes the math significantly. If you have a quieter month and only burn through 12 GB of your 20 GB pool, the remaining 8 GB rolls forward. Next month you effectively have 28 GB available. For crew who travel in bursts — heavy travel months followed by lighter ones — First Class builds a genuine data reserve.

First is the plan for crew who stream Netflix from Bangkok, video call in 4K from Frankfurt, and hotspot their laptop from the hotel in Sydney without worrying about the meter running.

**Monthly cost:** $44/month
**Data:** 20 GB pool
**Carryover:** 100% (indefinite, up to plan maximum)

**Best for:** Data-heavy crew, Netflix streamers, anyone who hotspots their laptop abroad.

---

### Cockpit — You. In Control.

Cockpit is CrewLink's annual plan, and it's built for crew who've made the calculation: I'm going to be flying for the next 12 months, so I should commit and save.

At $249 per year, Cockpit works out to $20.75 per month — saving you over $50 compared to paying Business month-to-month for a year, and more than $150 compared to twelve months of First Class.

Beyond the price, Cockpit has two features no other plan offers. First, you get 12 GB per month of pooled data, plus a 12 GB emergency bank — a separate reserve that refills annually, available any time you exceed your monthly pool. Second, carryover is 100% for up to two consecutive months: if your balance from month one rolls into month two, it stays alive for the full two months before any expiry.

Cockpit also comes with priority support, first access to new features, and — for founding members in the beta cohort — a permanent rate lock.

**Annual cost:** $249/year ($20.75/month equivalent)
**Data:** 12 GB/month + 12 GB emergency bank
**Carryover:** 100% for up to 2 months

**Best for:** Long-term crew who want the best value and don't want to think about their eSIM plan again for a year.

### Refuel — Out of Fuel? One Tap.

Refuel is not a plan — it's a top-up add-on available to any CrewLink user at any time.

If you're on Economy or Roster Bundle and you run out of data mid-trip, Refuel lets you purchase extra gigabytes on the spot. Standard pricing is $2.99 per GB. Cockpit members get a discounted rate of $1.99 per GB as a perk.

Refuel packages can be applied to any active eSIM in your account. There's no minimum purchase. If you just need 500 MB to get through an airport, that's not how Refuel is packaged — minimum is 1 GB — but for most situations, 1 GB is plenty of runway.

**Pricing:** $2.99/GB standard | $1.99/GB for Cockpit members
**Minimum:** 1 GB

**Best for:** Anyone who runs low mid-trip and needs a quick top-up without switching plans.

Total monthly cost: $5–7. No subscriptions, no overhead.

With Roster Bundle, Marco uploads his Crew Pulse screenshot. The app builds all three eSIMs automatically. He pays $18 (15% off the $20 individual total). He never opens the app again that month — everything activates when his plane lands.

Total monthly cost: ~$18–25 depending on destinations. Maximum convenience.

## How Refunds Work — The Six Scenarios

CrewLink's refund policy is the most crew-friendly in the eSIM industry. Here's every scenario explained plainly.

### Scenario 1: I Bought an eSIM But Didn't Activate It

This is the most common situation. You bought an Economy eSIM for your Singapore layover. Your flight got cancelled. The eSIM is sitting unused in your account.

You have 14 days from the purchase date to request a refund. You can get the full amount back to your original payment method, or — and this is the default because it's faster — you can take it as account credit, which never expires and applies to any future CrewLink purchase.

The app prompts you to choose when you tap "Refund." Most crew choose credit because it shows up instantly and they know they'll fly again soon.

**Result:** Full refund or full account credit. Your choice. 14-day window.

---

### Scenario 2: I Activated By Mistake

Accidents happen. You were on your phone at 5 AM before a briefing, you tapped the wrong button, and you activated a Thailand eSIM when your layover is actually in Hong Kong.

CrewLink allows 3 "oops moments" per calendar year. Within 15 minutes of activation, you can tap the "Undo Activation" button. The eSIM is retired. You get 50% of the purchase value back as account credit (the other 50% covers the supplier activation cost that's already been processed on our end — we can't recover that).

This isn't available at unlimited frequency — it would be abused. But 3 times per year is enough for genuine mistakes.

**Result:** 50% credit returned to your account. Limit: 3 times per calendar year.

---

### Scenario 3: I Bought a Bundle But Didn't Use All Destinations

This is specific to the Roster Bundle. Imagine you bought a 5-destination bundle for $39.99. Your schedule changed mid-month and you only visited 3 of the 5 countries.

CrewLink calculates the value of the 2 unused country packages on a pro-rata basis. In this example, 2 unactivated packages out of 5 = 40% of the bundle value. 40% of $39.99 = approximately $16. That $16 is returned to your account as credit.

The calculation uses the original per-country rates at time of purchase, not today's market rate. You'll always see the exact credit amount before confirming the refund.

**Result:** Pro-rata credit for each unused destination. Calculated transparently before you confirm.

---

### Scenario 4: I Want to Cancel My Monthly Subscription

For month-to-month subscriptions (Premium Economy, Business, First), there are no mid-month refunds. Your plan runs to the end of the current billing period, then cancels. You keep all data access until the period ends.

If you're an annual Cockpit member, you have a 14-day cooling-off window from the date of purchase to request a full refund. After 14 days, the annual plan is non-refundable — but EU and UK residents have extended rights under local consumer protection law (30 days for EU residents, 14 days + reason-based rights for UK residents). We honor those fully.

If you cancel Cockpit after the cooling-off period, you retain access for the remainder of the 12-month term. We don't cut off service on cancellation.

**Result:** Monthly plans end at period close. Annual plans: 14-day full refund. EU/UK: extended statutory rights honored.

---

### Scenario 5: The eSIM Didn't Work

This is a rare but serious situation. You landed in Tokyo, tried to activate your eSIM, and it simply didn't connect. Maybe the provider had an outage. Maybe there was a device compatibility issue we didn't catch.

CrewLink's policy here is unconditional: full refund, always, no questions asked. Additionally, you receive 2 GB of bonus credit added to your account as a courtesy, and a free replacement eSIM for the same country at no charge (where technically possible).

We will also follow up within 24 hours with an explanation of what went wrong. If it's a systemic issue, we proactively contact all affected crew members rather than waiting for them to complain.

**Result:** 100% refund + 2 GB bonus credit + free replacement. No questions asked. Always.

---

### Scenario 6: I Filed a Chargeback

We understand chargebacks — they exist because a lot of subscription companies make refunds needlessly hard. CrewLink's refund flow is designed so you never need to go to your bank.

If you file a chargeback, here's what happens on our end: we receive a fee from our payment processor (typically $15–25) regardless of the outcome. If the chargeback succeeds, we've still lost that fee plus the refund amount. This eats into the margins that allow us to keep pricing low for all crew members.

More practically: chargebacks suspend your account during the dispute period, which means no eSIM access while you're flying. Our refund process takes 3–7 days. A chargeback takes 4–8 weeks.

If you've hit a wall with a refund request and feel unheard, the right move is to email crew@crewlink.io with the subject line "Escalate" — we commit to a personal response within 12 hours and immediate supervisor review of your case.

**Result:** Chargebacks are slower for you and costly for us. Our in-app refund flow is always faster. Use it first.

## The Five Principles Behind CrewLink

These aren't marketing slogans. They're design decisions made during development that shape every feature and policy.

**1. We never store your Emirates ID.** When you verify as crew, we confirm your status with the airline's verification API and then immediately discard the credential. We store a hashed token that confirms you're verified — not who you are. Privacy-by-design.

**2. Pay-per-activation, not pay-per-purchase.** We only purchase data from our supplier when you actually activate an eSIM. If you buy an Economy pack and it sits unused for 13 days, we haven't bought anything on your behalf yet. This lets us offer 14-day unused refunds without absorbing supplier costs.

**3. Credit-first refunds.** Account credit is faster to process, never expires, and is more useful for crew who will definitely fly again. We default to credit — but always give you the cash option. No one should feel trapped.

**4. The cabin-class metaphor, everywhere.** Your plan name is your travel identity. If you're on Business, you're a working crew member who needs reliable, professional-grade connectivity. If you're on Cockpit, you're the one who's done the math and made the commitment. The metaphor isn't decoration — it's how we think about product tiers.

**5. Made by people who work with crew.** CrewLink was co-founded by someone with deep relationships inside the Emirates crew community. Every feature — Roster Bundle, carryover percentages, the "oops button," the pro-rata bundle refund — came from real conversations with real crew about what actually frustrated them.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What if my flight gets cancelled mid-roster-bundle?**
A: Unused destinations in your Roster Bundle are refundable on a pro-rata basis. If you never landed in Bangkok because the flight cancelled, that Bangkok package credit comes back to your account automatically when you request a review. Takes less than 48 hours.

**Q: Can I share my subscription with my husband or partner?**
A: Not currently — each CrewLink account is tied to one verified crew member, and eSIMs are installed on a single device. We're exploring a "household companion" add-on for beta Phase 2, but it's not available yet.

**Q: Do I need to install all eSIMs from a Roster Bundle at once?**
A: No. Your Roster Bundle generates one eSIM per destination country. You install them one at a time, and each activates on first use in that country. You can install all upfront if you prefer, or install them one by one as you land. Either works.

**Q: What if I'm not Emirates crew — can I still use CrewLink?**
A: Our beta launches with Emirates crew verification only. In Phase 2 (target Q3 2026), we expand to Etihad, flydubai, and JAL/ANA. If you're crew on another airline, you can join the waitlist at crewlink.io — we'll notify you when your airline is added.

**Q: How is CrewLink different from Airalo?**
A: Airalo is a marketplace connecting you with local operators worldwide. CrewLink is a curated, crew-optimized service: we vet every provider, build the Roster Bundle feature specifically for monthly schedules, offer carryover data that Airalo doesn't, and price for crew volume rather than tourist one-offs. Airalo is great for occasional travellers. CrewLink is purpose-built for professionals.

**Q: What happens to my unused GB if I cancel my subscription?**
A: For monthly plans, your data remains available until your current billing period ends, then expires. For Cockpit, your data stays live for the remainder of your 12-month term. There's no mid-period cutoff.

**Q: Does CrewLink work on all phones?**
A: CrewLink requires eSIM-compatible hardware. All iPhone XS and newer models are supported. Most flagship Android devices from 2020 onward support eSIM. We have a compatibility checker in the app — just tap "Check my device" during onboarding.

**Q: I'm already using Airalo. How do I switch?**
A: You don't need to do anything with existing Airalo eSIMs — they live in separate app profiles on your device. Just download CrewLink, verify as crew, and start a parallel CrewLink eSIM alongside any existing ones. Most crew run CrewLink alongside other eSIMs for their first month, then switch fully once they see the Roster Bundle working.

**Q: What's the emergency bank on Cockpit, and when does it reset?**
A: The 12 GB emergency bank is separate from your monthly 12 GB pool. You draw on it when your monthly pool hits zero. It refills once per year on your Cockpit anniversary date, not monthly. Think of it as a safety net for your two or three heaviest travel months of the year.

**Q: What countries does CrewLink cover?**
A: At launch, CrewLink covers 87 countries — every country in Emirates' current route network plus the top 15 additional destinations where Emirates crew commonly travel on personal trips. Full coverage map is at crewlink.io/coverage.

**Q: What if I have a technical problem during a flight or late at night?**
A: In-app AI chat is available 24/7 for install questions, top-up requests, and basic troubleshooting. For refund disputes or account issues that require human review, email crew@crewlink.io — our target first-response time is 12 hours for standard queries, 4 hours for Cockpit members.

**Q: Can I use my Peanut Points for a free eSIM?**
A: Yes. Peanut Points accumulate every time you activate an eSIM, refer a crew friend, or contribute a Crew Rec review. 500 points = 1 GB free data applied to your next Refuel. Full redemption details are in the app under "My Points."

**Q: What currency are all prices in?**
A: All prices are in USD. We display AED equivalents in the app for UAE-based crew (refreshed daily at the live exchange rate).

**Q: I found a cheaper eSIM somewhere else. Will you match it?**
A: We don't do price matching, but we're confident our total-cost comparison holds up when you factor in Roster Bundle discounts, carryover, and not having to manually manage per-country purchases. If you find a better deal for your specific flying pattern, we'd genuinely like to know — email us and we'll review our pricing for that route.
