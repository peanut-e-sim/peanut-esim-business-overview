Three ways to buy data.
Only one is active right now.

We modeled three supply chain options — A (pay-per-order, current baseline), B (bulk prepay for a discount), C (direct wholesale bypass). Options B and C are placeholders until we negotiate real quotes. The strategic path: launch on A, grow to 1,500+ users (the negotiation-ready threshold), then evaluate switching to Option C — which only wins above the 1,724-user crossover.

Why does Option C look worse at 1,000 users? The $5,000/month platform fee is a fixed overhead that gets divided across active users. At 1,000 users it costs $5/user — wiping out most of the 40% COGS saving. This math flips at the crossover (Section 3 below) when the platform fee spread drops to the per-user savings level.

3The crossover: when Option C starts winning

Option C saves $4.86/user blended across all tiers vs Option A on data COGS (rebuilt 2026-06-02 with corrected rate sheet; old $2.90 at invalidated $1.34/GB). 4The strategic procurement plan

We do not negotiate from a position of weakness. Volume comes first, leverage follows.

What we discussed and rejected: Moving immediately to direct wholesale (Option C) was considered. Rejected because (1) we have no volume leverage at launch, (2) direct providers require due diligence and contract time, (3) the $5K/month platform fee is painful at low user counts. Wholesalers don't drop prices voluntarily — leverage must be earned through volume, competition awareness, or demonstrated alternatives.
