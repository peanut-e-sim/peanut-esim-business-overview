2 · Refund policy (6 scenarios — symmetric across all tiers)

Math formula: bundle_price × (unused / total) = credit. Predictable, simple, always applied.

1
Unused pool → pro-rata refund
Within 7 days of plan end. Unused GB × per-GB rate refunded back to original card.
2
Install failed (Apple-side)
Full refund, no questions. We log the failure for upstream debugging.
3
Carrier connectivity failure
Credit OR refund at user's choice. We verify the connectivity failure first.
4
Cancel before activation
Full refund, no questions. Most common refund reason — fastest path.
5
First 24h, <50 MB consumed
Full refund, no questions. Generous trial-window — better than chargeback risk.
6
Subscription downgrade mid-month
Pro-rata credit toward next month. No cash refund needed for downgrades.

4 · Upgrade nudges (built into the app, fired automatically)

  • After 3 months on Business: "You've been flying Business 3 months. Step into the Cockpit — same data, $50 off the year."
  • 90% pool used on Premium Economy: "Pool running low? Upgrade to Business for more headroom — or Refuel for $2.99."
  • Cockpit confirmation: "Welcome to the Cockpit. You're flying this for the year."
  • Empty pool on Premium Economy: "Premium Economy is great for messaging — heavy streaming might leave you stranded. Upgrade to Business or Refuel anytime."
  • Empty pool on First: "First is built to handle this — Refuel if you really need more."
  • Empty pool on Cockpit: "You're in the Cockpit — you have the 12 GB emergency bank to dip into."
Why this structure works: Crew don't compare data + carryover + minute counts in a spreadsheet. They identify with a cabin. Pick "Business" → done. Every nudge speaks the same metaphor. Refund rules are universal so nobody has to read tier-specific fine print. The 7 tiers fit on one screen.
