05 · Top 5 risks

What could kill this.

  • 1

    Founder agreement gap (Balraj + Akito)

    Operating today on goodwill and shared text threads. The longer this runs without paper, the more painful any disagreement becomes — and the harder it gets to walk into Stripe Atlas with clean ownership. Mitigation: start as single-member LLC under Balraj, formalize equity once founder agreement is negotiated (Phase 0 Item 5 path).

  • 2

    Single-customer-segment dependency (Emirates crew)

    If Emirates objects to the crew-gating mechanic (Phase 1 Item 18 — crew verification legal framework), they could effectively shut us out of our day-1 audience. Mitigation: build crew-verification on the lowest-friction option (airline email domain) so we never touch identity documents; have JAL / ANA / Singapore expansion path ready before opening any conversation with Emirates HR.

  • 3

    Aggregator concentration (Samurai is Tier-3 sub-reseller)

    Per the 40%-markup audit, Samurai sits 3 hops down the food chain — we're paying a stacked margin and inheriting their pricing volatility. If they walk or change terms, the whole catalog breaks. Mitigation: Decision 03 (aggregator strategy) closes only after we have 3+ real direct quotes; eSIM Access is the leading alternative.

  • 4

    Apple / Google policy risk (in-app eSIM, IAP coercion)

    Apple's carrier entitlement is gatekept and political; Apple could also push us onto IAP at the 30% rate (Decision 01). Either alone is painful, both together is fatal. Mitigation: Netflix-hybrid model — web checkout primary, app for browse + install only. Apple Carrier Entitlement applied post-launch (Phase 1 Item 16) once we have user numbers to justify.

  • 5

    Crew SIM accelerates / Airalo notices

    Crew SIM is solo + slow today, but a price drop or a copycat schedule-upload feature could erase our wedge in weeks. Bigger threat: Airalo (the category leader) decides crew is worth a vertical and ships it themselves with their distribution. Mitigation: depth-over-breadth — build crew-vertical features Airalo's horizontal positioning makes structurally awkward to ship (roster integration, crew-only Michelin recs layer).
