01 — THE FOOD CHAIN

Four tiers. You sit on the third.

eSIM connectivity travels through four hands before reaching the user's phone. Knowing who owns what makes everything downstream make sense.

i

Tier 1 · InfrastructureMobile Network Operators

They own the actual towers and spectrum. Vodafone, AT&T, Etisalat, NTT Docomo, KDDI, T-Mobile, Orange. They sell data wholesale, mostly in bulk to roaming partners. They do not talk to consumers like you directly.

ii

Tier 2 · AggregatoreSIM aggregators

The middleman layer. They've stitched together wholesale roaming deals with hundreds of MNOs and run the technical plumbing (SM-DP+ servers, billing, eSIM profiles). They expose all of that to you as a single API. Names: 1GLOBAL, eSIM Go, Telna, Truphone, MobileX — and Akito's Thailand contact.

★

Tier 3 · Reseller / Brand · YOUBrand-facing app

You wrap an aggregator's API in a polished, branded experience. You own the customer relationship, the marketing, the support, the price. The aggregator is invisible to your user. Airalo, Holafly, Nomad sit here — and so will you.

iv

Tier 4 · CustomerThe traveler

Opens your app, picks a country, pays. Installs the eSIM in seconds. Flies somewhere. Stays connected. Never knew there were three companies behind the experience — and that's exactly the point.

02 — THE API SURFACE

The whole API is genuinely just four things.

When people say "we'll plug into an eSIM API", this is what they actually mean. Four endpoints. That's the entire game.

GET /packages

Catalog

Every country, every plan, every wholesale price. You pull this on a schedule and present it in your app — usually with your own markup on top.

"Here's what's for sale today."
POST /orders

Order

The one transactional call. You pay the aggregator wholesale, they reserve an eSIM profile for your user. This is the call that costs you money each time it fires.

"Charge me. Give me an eSIM."
GET /esim/install

Install method

A QR code, an LPA activation string, or a universal link. Your app shows this to the user right after checkout. This is the moment of truth — make it feel effortless.

"Here's how to put me on your phone."
GET /esim/usage

Usage

How much data has this eSIM consumed. Your app polls this to show users a live "2.4 GB of 5 GB used" bar — table-stakes UX in this category.

"How much have they burned?"

Where every dollar of a $15 plan actually goes.

The Apple-tax escape hatch. Route checkout to a web page instead of using in-app purchase and that $4.50 stays yours — margin jumps from $5.05 to $9.55 on the same sale. Apple has been cracking down on this since the Epic ruling; some operators still do it, but it's a moving target. Airalo just eats the 30% to avoid the fight. Decision worth a separate conversation.
04 — THE MAGIC

How an eSIM actually lands on a phone.

Strip away the marketing and an eSIM is just a small file. It's downloaded from a special server the aggregator runs, called an SM-DP+. The user reaches that server in one of three ways.

🛰

SM-DP+

SUBSCRIPTION MANAGER
DATA PREPARATION

A

Scan a QR code

The old reliable. The QR encodes the SM-DP+ address + activation token. User opens their camera, taps "add eSIM", done. Works on every eSIM-capable phone since 2018.

UNIVERSAL · FRICTION: 4 TAPS
B

Tap a universal link

iOS 17.4+ and modern Android. User taps a link inside your app (or in an email) and the eSIM installs in one tap. This is the killer modern UX.

PREMIUM · FRICTION: 1 TAP
C

In-app install (carrier entitlement)

The eSIM installs without the user ever leaving your app. Requires Apple's com.apple.CommCenter.fine-grained entitlement — granted only to approved carriers. Airalo has it. You won't on day one. Apply later, once you have scale.

REQUIRES APPLE APPROVAL
